Planning & Bookings

Essential bookings and strategic planning for Iceland 2026

Priority Bookings (Book ASAP)

1. Blue Lagoon Entry

Status: NOT BOOKED

Date: Day 9 (April 16) - Afternoon slot recommended (15:30-16:00)

Why urgent: Books out weeks/months in advance, especially peak season

Packages:

  • Comfort Package: Entry + towel + drink (~$80 USD)
  • Premium Package: Entry + robe + slippers + mask + drink (~$110 USD)

Booking: bluelagoon.com

Tip: Book at least 2-3 months in advance. Afternoon slot gives you time to drive from Snaefellsnes.

2. Rental Car

Status: NOT BOOKED

Duration: 10 days (April 8-17, pickup/drop-off at Keflavik Airport)

Vehicle Type: 2WD sufficient (all paved roads), but 4WD recommended for April weather

Why urgent: Prices increase closer to trip date

Recommended Companies:

  • SADcars - Budget friendly, good reviews
  • Blue Car Rental - Mid-range (mentioned in plan)
  • Hertz/Budget - International chains

Insurance: Consider gravel protection & sand/ash damage coverage

3. Optional: Glacier Hiking or Ice Cave Tour

Duration: 3-5 hours

Companies:

  • Guide to Iceland: Various tour operators
  • Local Guide: Glacier hiking specialists

Price: ~$100-150 USD per person

Note: New plan doesn't include North Iceland/Husavik, so whale watching tour not applicable

Packing Guide for April

April Weather Reality

Temperature: 0-5C (32-41F) with wind chill significantly below freezing

Conditions: Mix of rain, sleet, occasional snow. "Horizontal rain" is real!

Vegetation: Pre-green landscape - mossy lava fields still vibrant green

Essential Clothing (Layers System)

Base Layer
  • Merino wool or synthetic thermal tops
  • Thermal leggings
  • Moisture-wicking socks (multiple pairs!)
Mid Layer
  • Fleece jacket or down vest
  • Wool sweater (Icelandic lopapeysa!)
  • Softshell hiking pants
Outer Layer
  • Waterproof shell jacket (CRITICAL!)
  • Waterproof pants (for waterfalls)
  • Warm hat, gloves, buff/scarf
Footwear
  • Waterproof hiking boots (broken in!)
  • Micro-spikes/crampons (for icy paths)
  • Waterproof gaiters (optional)

Photography Gear (April Edition)

April light is crisp with extended golden hours. The sun doesn't rise high, creating long shadows perfect for textured landscapes.

Camera & Lenses

  • Wide Angle (14-24mm) - Essential for waterfalls, ice caves
  • Telephoto (70-200mm) - Church/mountain compression, seals at Ytri-Tunga
  • Weather-sealed camera body preferred

Filters & Accessories

  • ND Filter (6-stop) - Essential for silky waterfall shots
  • Polarizer - Mandatory! Cuts wet rock glare, deepens blue ice/sky
  • Sturdy tripod with hook (hang bag for wind stability)

Protection (Critical!)

  • Many microfiber cloths - Waterfall spray is constant
  • Rain cover for camera
  • Extra batteries (cold drains them fast)
  • Keep lens cap on until you shoot!
Drone Rules

Drones are BANNED in National Parks (Thingvellir, Snaefellsjokull, Vatnajokull) and at major tourist sites (Skogafoss, Gullfoss) without special permit.

Pre-Trip Checklist

3 Months Before

  • Book Blue Lagoon entry (Day 9, April 16)
  • Book rental car with insurance (April 8-17)
  • Research and book accommodations (all 9 nights)
  • Book optional glacier hiking tour if desired
  • Purchase travel insurance
  • Book Reykjadalur hot river hike slot if required

1 Month Before

  • Book optional glacier tours if desired
  • Download offline maps (maps.me or Google Maps)
  • Download Aurora forecast app
  • Create detailed day-by-day Google Maps with pins
  • Check weather patterns and pack accordingly

1 Week Before

  • Check all confirmation emails
  • Download offline maps for entire route
  • Print rental car voucher
  • Bring credit card for car rental deposit
  • Pack layers (waterproof, fleece, base layers)
  • Check Iceland road conditions website (road.is)

Money-Saving Tips

  • Cook some meals (grocery shop at Bonus supermarkets)
  • Fill up water bottles (Iceland tap water is pristine)
  • Stay in guesthouses or hostels instead of hotels
  • Visit free attractions (most nature sites have no entry fee)
  • Pack snacks and lunch for driving days
