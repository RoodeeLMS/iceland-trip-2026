Temperature: 0-5C (32-41F) with wind chill significantly below freezing

Vegetation: Pre-green landscape - mossy lava fields still vibrant green

Photography Gear (April Edition)

April light is crisp with extended golden hours. The sun doesn't rise high, creating long shadows perfect for textured landscapes.

Camera & Lenses

  • Wide Angle (14-24mm) - Essential for waterfalls, ice caves
  • Telephoto (70-200mm) - Church/mountain compression, seals at Ytri-Tunga
  • Weather-sealed camera body preferred

Filters & Accessories

  • ND Filter (6-stop) - Essential for silky waterfall shots
  • Polarizer - Mandatory! Cuts wet rock glare, deepens blue ice/sky
  • Sturdy tripod with hook (hang bag for wind stability)

Protection (Critical!)

  • Many microfiber cloths - Waterfall spray is constant
  • Rain cover for camera
  • Extra batteries (cold drains them fast)
  • Keep lens cap on until you shoot!

🚁 Drone Usage in Iceland (DJI Neo 2)

Nick is bringing a DJI Neo 2 (sub-250g class). Iceland has strict drone rules — read this before your first flight. Source: Icelandic Transport Authority (Samgöngustofa) and safetravel.is.

✅ Operator registration complete
Operator number: ISLmded1vbae2qq9-tuu · Valid until 06/04/2031 · Issued via flydrone.is. Affix this ID to the drone before flying.

⚖️ Legal requirements (EU A1 class, <250g)

  • Registration: ✅ Done — operator ID above. Valid across all EU/EASA countries for 5 years.
  • No pilot license needed for <250g in the A1 "open" category — but you must follow the rules below.
  • Max altitude: 120m (400ft) above ground level.
  • Visual line of sight (VLOS) at all times. No FPV goggles unless a second observer is present.
  • Insurance: Third-party liability insurance is recommended (some travel insurance covers it — check your PLATINUM policy).

🚫 No-fly zones (important!)

  • National parks: Thingvellir, Vatnajökull (Skaftafell, Jokulsarlon area), and Snæfellsjökull National Park all require a permit for drone use. Without one = illegal.
  • Airports: 2km radius from Keflavík (KEF), Reykjavík domestic (RKV), and all regional airfields. Check the official drone map.
  • Over crowds / people: Never fly over uninvolved people. Stay 50m+ from people, vehicles, and buildings not under your control.
  • Bird nesting areas: Dyrholaey is closed to drones May 1 – Jun 25 (puffin nesting). Other cliffs similarly restricted during nesting season — April is mostly safe.
  • Reynisfjara: On-site "No Drones" signage (bird sanctuary + crowd safety). Do not fly here.
  • Blue Lagoon: No drones over the lagoon or resort.
  • Active volcanic / eruption sites: Temporary flight restrictions (TFRs) apply — check vedur.is and NOTAMs before flying anywhere on the Reykjanes peninsula.

💨 Weather limits for DJI Neo 2

  • Wind resistance: ~8 m/s (Level 4). Iceland regularly has 10-20 m/s winds — check the wind forecast before EVERY flight.
  • At 10 m/s+ sustained: DO NOT FLY. Neo 2 will struggle to return home and may be lost.
  • At 6-8 m/s: Fly with extreme caution. Take off facing into the wind. Keep the drone close.
  • Gusts: Pay attention to wind_gusts_10m_max on the forecast page — gusts can be 50% higher than sustained.
  • Temperature: Neo 2 operating range 0-40°C. April Iceland nights can drop to -5°C — warm the battery in your pocket before flight. Expect reduced battery life.
  • Rain/snow: Neo 2 is NOT waterproof. Even light drizzle can ruin it. Check precipitation before flying.
  • Geomagnetic storms (aurora): High Kp can interfere with GPS/compass. Avoid flying during aurora storms (Kp 5+).

✅ Good drone spots on our itinerary

  • Day 3 — Seljalandsfoss area (away from the waterfall crowds): decent for waterfall-from-above shots if wind allows.
  • Day 4 — Fjadrargljufur Canyon: spectacular aerial. Check for current restrictions.
  • Day 4 — Vestrahorn / Stokksnes: iconic drone location, private beach (pay fee at the café). Less crowded than Ring Road sights.
  • Day 6 — Hraunfossar: aerial shows the lava-falls pattern beautifully.
  • Day 7 — Arnarstapi coast / Londrangar: dramatic cliffs, good drone territory outside the main viewpoints.
  • Day 8 — Kirkjufell: CLASSIC shot. Fly EARLY (before crowds arrive) and check wind carefully — the area is exposed.
  • Day 9 — Kleifarvatn Lake: quiet, often windless-ish, no crowds.

📋 Pre-flight checklist (every time)

  1. Check wind speed + gusts on forecast page (all 4 weather models).
  2. Check for active weather warnings on safetravel.is.
  3. Verify location is NOT in a national park or no-fly zone.
  4. Check for "No Drones" signage on arrival.
  5. Warm battery in pocket (cold Iceland air = 30% battery loss).
  6. Confirm GPS lock before takeoff (Iceland's northern latitude = weaker GPS sometimes).
  7. Set Return-to-Home altitude ABOVE nearby cliffs/terrain.
  8. Keep VLOS — Iceland's clear air makes the drone look closer than it is.
⚠️ When in doubt — don't fly. Iceland fines for illegal drone use can exceed ฿150,000 and confiscation is possible. The photos aren't worth it. Also: downed drones in lava fields or glaciers are unrecoverable.
