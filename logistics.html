Rental Car Strategy

Vehicle Requirements

Recommended: 2WD sufficient for our route (all paved roads)

Upgrade Option: 4WD/AWD for better handling in April weather

Size: Small SUV or larger for comfort and 4 passengers with luggage

Why 4WD Can Be Beneficial:

  • Better handling in April weather (potential snow, wind)
  • Safer in inclement weather conditions
  • Better stability on gravel parking areas
  • Peace of mind for spring conditions

Note for Our Route:

  • All destinations accessible via paved Route 1 and maintained roads
  • No F-roads or highland driving required
  • April weather can be variable - 4WD recommended but not essential

Essential Insurance & Add-ons

Gravel Protection (GP/SCDW)

Cost: ~$15-20/day

Why: Covers chips from flying gravel (extremely common in Iceland)

Verdict: HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Sand & Ash Protection (SAAP)

Cost: ~$10-15/day

Why: Covers damage from volcanic sand/ash storms

Verdict: RECOMMENDED

Collision Damage Waiver (CDW)

Cost: Usually included, or ~$20-30/day

Why: Basic protection, but high deductible

Verdict: ESSENTIAL (often mandatory)

Super CDW

Cost: ~$25-35/day

Why: Reduces deductible significantly

Verdict: OPTIONAL (depends on risk tolerance)

Theft Protection

Cost: ~$5-10/day

Why: Iceland has very low crime rate

Verdict: NOT NECESSARY

Fuel Strategy

Cost: ~$2.20-2.50 per liter (~$8.50-9.50/gallon)

Tank Size: Typical SUV ~60L (16 gallons)

Full Tank Cost: ~$130-150 USD

Estimated Total: $250-350 for ~1,000km trip (less than full Ring Road)

Gas Station Tips:

  • N1, Orkan, OB, Olis: Major chains across Iceland
  • Self-service: Most stations require credit card with PIN (Visa/Mastercard)
  • Strategic fill-ups: Top up before heading to Jokulsarlon and when entering Snaefellsnes
  • Apps: Download N1 or Orkan apps for station locations

Driving Conditions & Safety

Iceland Driving Challenges

Wind (Most Common Hazard)

Risk: Extremely strong gusts can blow car doors off or push vehicles off road

Solution:

  • Hold door firmly when opening in wind
  • Park facing away from wind when possible
  • Drive slower on exposed sections (South Coast, highlands)
  • Check wind forecasts daily (vedur.is)

Sheep on Roads

Risk: Free-roaming sheep, especially in summer

Solution: Drive slower on rural roads, always be ready to brake

Single-Lane Bridges

Risk: Common throughout Iceland, yield rules apply

Solution: Slow down, first to bridge has right of way

Gravel Roads & F-Roads

Risk: Unpaved roads, flying gravel, river crossings

Solution:

  • Slow down to 60-70 km/h on gravel
  • F-roads require 4WD and are closed until mid-June
  • Never attempt river crossings without experience

Midnight Sun (Summer)

Risk: 24-hour daylight can disrupt sleep and sense of time

Solution: Bring eye mask, set alarms, don't rely on darkness cues

Essential Resources

Important Rules:

  • Headlights: Required 24/7 year-round
  • Seatbelts: Mandatory for all passengers
  • Off-road driving: Strictly illegal (hefty fines + environmental damage)
  • Alcohol limit: 0.05% BAC (0.02% for new drivers)
  • Mobile phones: Hands-free only
  • Speed cameras: Common, automatic fines sent to rental companies

Parking & Practical Tips

Parking

  • Tourist sites: Usually free, but parking lots may be gravel
  • Reykjavik: Paid parking downtown (use app: Parka or Easypark)
  • Blue Lagoon: Free parking with reservation
  • Jokulsarlon: Large free parking lot

General Driving Tips

  • Distances deceiving: 100km can take 2+ hours on winding roads
  • Stop frequently: Every viewpoint is photo-worthy
  • Fuel up proactively: Long stretches between stations
  • Check weather daily: Can change rapidly
  • Respect nature: Stay on marked paths, close gates, take trash
  • Plan for delays: You'll want to stop more than expected

The "Food Desert" Warning

For 7 adults, food is the largest variable cost. Relying on gas station burgers ($20 USD/ea) is unsustainable. Plan your grocery stops strategically!

Money-Saving Food Tips

  • Duty-Free Alcohol: Buy in the baggage claim area immediately upon arrival at KEF - it's significantly cheaper than state-run stores (Vinbudin)
  • Tap Water: Iceland's tap water is among the purest in the world - fill reusable bottles!
  • Picnic Lunches: Buy bread, cheese, and deli meats for roadside lunches
  • Skyr: Icelandic yogurt - cheap, filling, protein-rich breakfast option
  • Hot Dogs: Icelandic pylsur (hot dogs) from gas stations are excellent and affordable (~$4-5)

Alternative Transport (If Not Renting Car)

Guided Tours

Companies: Reykjavik Excursions, Gray Line Iceland, Arctic Adventures

Pros: No driving stress, guides provide information, meet other travelers

Cons: More expensive, fixed schedules, less flexibility, tourist-heavy

Popular Tours:

  • Golden Circle Day Tour (~$60-80)
  • South Coast Tour (~$100-130)
  • Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon Tour (~$150-200)

Public Bus System

Company: Straeto (local buses)

Reality: Very limited outside Reykjavik area, impractical for Ring Road

Verdict: Not recommended for our itinerary

Why Self-Drive is Best for This Trip

  • Flexibility to stop at viewpoints and hidden spots
  • Go at your own pace (no rushing for tour groups)
  • Access to remote areas and off-the-beaten-path locations
  • Can adjust plans based on weather
  • More cost-effective for 10-day Ring Road circuit
  • Privacy and comfort
