Hidden Gems & Local Secrets

Carefully researched spots to escape the crowds and discover the real Iceland.

Bruarfoss (Blue Waterfall)

Electric blue water churning through a black volcanic slot canyon. New parking lot makes it a 3km round trip walk. Best kept secret of the Golden Circle!

Gljufrabui (Canyon Dweller)

Hidden waterfall 500m from Seljalandsfoss. Wade through a shallow stream into a slot canyon where a waterfall crashes into a mossy chamber. Bring waterproof everything!

Kvernufoss

Behind Skogar Museum - same walk-behind experience as Seljalandsfoss but with 5% of the visitors. The view looking out from behind the waterfall is spectacular.

Mulagljufur Canyon

Alternative to crowded Fjadrargljufur. 45-60 min hike to a majestic canyon with Hangandifoss waterfall. Often completely empty!

Raudfeldsgja Gorge

A narrow "Indiana Jones" crack in the mountain on Snaefellsnes. You can walk inside! Note: May have ice/snow in April.

Selvallafoss (Sheep's Waterfall)

Unmarked pullout on Route 56 (Snaefellsnes). Walk 5 mins to a waterfall you can walk behind - often completely deserted!

üì∏ Photography Tips

April Light Quality

Extended golden hours 06:00-08:00 & 19:30-21:00. Low sun = long shadows and textured landscapes.

Essential Gear

Wide angle (14-24mm), telephoto (70-200mm), ND filter (6-stop), polarizer (cuts wet rock glare), many microfiber cloths!

Drone Rules

BANNED in National Parks (Thingvellir, Snaefellsjokull, Vatnajokull) and major tourist sites without permit.

Spray Warning

Skogafoss & Gljufrabui: Intense spray! Keep lens cap on until you shoot.
