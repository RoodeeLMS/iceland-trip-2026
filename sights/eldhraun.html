Eldhraun Lava Field

Day 4
📍 Coordinates63.7536, -17.9772
📅 Trip DayDay 4
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Eldhraun is the largest lava flow on Earth from a single eruption in recorded history — 565 km² of lava produced by the catastrophic Laki fissure eruption of 1783-1784, which killed roughly a quarter of Iceland's population through famine and poisoning and lowered global temperatures for years. Today the field is blanketed in a thick, soft layer of woolly fringe moss (Racomitrium lanuginosum), a unique ecosystem that takes decades to centuries to recover from a single footstep. Route 1 cuts straight through it on the drive between Vik and Kirkjubaejarklaustur.

🚦 Current Status

🚫 DO NOT WALK ON THE MOSS
This is the most important rule on the south coast. The moss is extremely fragile — a single footprint can leave a visible scar for 10-50+ years. The 2019 Justin Bieber music video shoot caused widespread damage to the moss here, and the site remains in recovery. Stay on the designated boardwalk and parking pull-off. Iceland actively fines visitors for off-trail walking on protected moss.

April 2026 conditions: Snow patches may remain in shaded hollows. The moss is still recovering from winter — even more fragile than in summer. The boardwalk is open year-round.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Pull off at the designated rest areaDriving Route 1 east from Vik (~50 km), watch for the signed pull-off and boardwalk entry near coordinates 63.7536, -17.9772. There are a couple of pull-offs in the area — the main one has a wooden boardwalk and information panels. Free parking.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Designated rest area, Route 1
2
Walk the boardwalk onlyCritical: Stay on the wooden boardwalk. Do not step onto the moss for any reason — not for a photo, not to "just touch it." The boardwalk gives you a complete view of the moss-covered lava in every direction.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Boardwalk
3
Read the information panelsInformation panels at the boardwalk explain the 1783 Laki eruption — the scale of destruction, the global climate impact, and why the moss is protected. Worth the 5 minutes; transforms the visit from "green rocks" to "site of one of the most consequential eruptions in human history."
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Info panels
4
Photograph the moss landscape from the boardwalkFrom the boardwalk you have all the angles you need. Wide for the rolling sea of green; tight for the moss texture detail. Resist the urge to step off for a "better angle" — there isn't one worth the damage.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Boardwalk vantage points
5
Continue driving — viewing from the roadAfter leaving the rest area, the next ~20 km of Route 1 cuts directly through the lava field. You can see it from the windows for the entire stretch. Total stop: 20-30 minutes is plenty.
⏱️ Drive · 📍 Route 1 east

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Wide moss-and-mountains: 24-35 mm from the boardwalk facing inland — moss field foreground, distant volcanic ridges background. Soft overcast light brings out the moss color best; harsh sun blows out the green.
  • Texture detail: 50-85 mm tighter shots of moss-covered lava forms. Look for "lava bombs" wearing moss caps.
  • Person on boardwalk for scale: Shows the boardwalk and human scale against the field.
  • Best light: Overcast or golden hour. Midday harsh sun is the worst.
  • 🚫 Drone: Discouraged here — the area is environmentally sensitive and busy with traffic. Some operators say drones are technically permitted away from the road, but the responsible call is to skip drones at Eldhraun. If you must, do so far from the boardwalk and from any people. See Planning page for general rules.
  • 🚫 Absolutely do not step on the moss for any photo, ever.

🚗 Getting There

Direct on Route 1, ~50 km east of Vik and ~10 km west of Kirkjubaejarklaustur. The signed rest area with the boardwalk is the legitimate stop. Paved highway, any car.

  • Parking: free at the rest area pull-off.
  • No entry fee.
  • No facilities at the pull-off — use Kirkjubaejarklaustur (10 km east).

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Quick 20-30 min stop on Day 4 driving east toward Skaftafell/Vatnajokull. Pair with Fjadrargljufur (~15 min farther east), Dverghamrar (5 min east) and Kirkjubaejarklaustur (lunch/fuel).

  • Best light: Overcast or soft morning/evening light for the moss colors.
  • Crowds: Light. Many tour buses drive through but don't stop.
  • Combine with: Dverghamrar, Fjadrargljufur, Kirkjubaejarklaustur — all clustered within 15 km.
  • Time budget: 20-30 min.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.6. Reviews focus on the unique alien-landscape feel and the historical significance once people learn about Laki. Common complaint: visitors who didn't know about the moss-protection rule and saw others walking off-trail.

Honest take: A high-impact stop relative to the time required. The moss-covered lava field has no real visual equivalent anywhere else on Earth, and the Laki history makes it more than just "green rocks." Just be the people who stay on the boardwalk.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities. The Laki crater row itself (the source of the eruption) is up an F-road and only accessible to 4x4 vehicles in summer; not feasible on this trip.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Stay on the boardwalk. The moss is the protected feature. Damage is illegal and visible for decades.
  • Watch for traffic at the highway pull-off — Route 1 traffic moves fast.
  • Loose lava rock at the boardwalk edge can roll under foot.
