Djúpalónssandur Pebble Beach

Day 7
📍 Coordinates64.7508, -23.9056
📅 Trip DayDay 7
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Djúpalónssandur is a black pebble beach inside Snæfellsjökull National Park, reached by a short walk down through tortured lava formations. Two things make it more than "another black beach": (1) the four historic lifting stones that 19th-century fishermen used to test strength for ship-crew jobs, and (2) the rusting iron remains of the British trawler Epine GY7, wrecked here in 1948 with the loss of 14 lives. The wreck pieces are scattered along the high tide line and are protected as a memorial — look, do not touch or move.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free, in Snæfellsjökull National Park. Sneaker-wave hazard same as all south-facing Iceland beaches: stay well back from the surf.

April 2026: Path to the beach can be icy in the lava section. Wind here is famously brutal — Djúpalónssandur is one of the windiest spots on the peninsula. Check forecast.is and skip in red wind warnings. The beach is in the national park where drones require a permit.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the Djúpalónssandur lotSignposted off Route 574 on the western tip of Snæfellsnes. Free gravel lot, ~30 spaces, with toilets (small fee). Information board at the trailhead.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Trailhead lot, 64.7508, -23.9056
2
Walk the path down through the lavaA 300 m gravel path winds down through twisted black lava pinnacles. Look for two named formations marked on the info board: Gatklettur (a small natural arch) and Söngklettur ("singing rock"). Path drops ~30 m to the beach. Easy walk, slippery if icy.
⏱️ 5-7 min · 📍 300 m, -30 m elevation
3
Find the four lifting stonesAt the back of the beach, four worn round basalt stones sit on a flat rock platform with their names inscribed: Fullsterkur (Full strength, 154 kg), Hálfsterkur (Half strength, 100 kg), Hálfdrættingur (Weakling, 54 kg), and Amlóði (Useless, 23 kg). To qualify as a deckhand, a man had to lift Hálfdrættingur to a hip-high ledge. You can try them yourself — most visitors can manage Amlóði and Hálfdrættingur. Lift safely: legs not back, do not drop on toes.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Lifting stones platform
4
Walk slowly along the wreck of the Epine GY7Scan the high-tide line for rusted iron plates, rivets, hull fragments, and a gear wheel. Pieces are spread over ~200 m. Memorial — do not pick up, move, or take. A small plaque near the path commemorates the 14 fishermen who died in the 1948 grounding.
⏱️ 10-15 min · 📍 Along the high tide line
5
Stay back from the surfSouth-facing beach with sneaker-wave risk identical to Reynisfjara. Stay at least 25 m from the water. The pebbles are large, smooth and rolly — easy to twist an ankle near the wave line.
⏱️ — · 📍 Along the beach
6
Walk back via the same pathReturn up the lava path to the lot. Optional 5-min detour: a small viewpoint south of the lot looks back over the cove.
⏱️ 7 min · 📍 Back to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

  • Lifting stones: Top-down 35-50 mm, all four in a row with their carved names visible. Late afternoon side-light reveals texture.
  • Wreck pieces: 50 mm or short tele tight on rusted iron with black pebbles as background. Strong colour contrast.
  • Lava-frame the beach: Ultra-wide 14-20 mm on the path down — frame the cove between two black lava pinnacles.
  • Best light: Late afternoon — sun is low over the western Atlantic.

🚁 Drone — PERMIT REQUIRED: Djúpalónssandur is inside Snæfellsjökull National Park. Per the park rules (snaefellsjokull.is / Umhverfisstofnun), drones require an explicit permit from the park warden — no casual flying. Treat as no-fly unless you have arranged a permit in advance. See Planning drone section.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 574 around the western tip, signed turnoff. Any rental car. ~30 min west of Arnarstapi.

  • Parking: Free, ~30 spaces.
  • Walk: 300 m down a lava path, 5-7 min, ~30 m elevation drop (and back up).
  • Fees: None for the beach. Toilet ~฿100.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 45-60 min including walk down and back.
  • Best light: Late afternoon — west-facing beach.
  • Combine with: Lóndrangar (10 min east) and Saxhóll (15 min north) on the Day 7 western tip loop.
  • Skip if: Red wind warning. The exposed beach is no fun in 25+ m/s gusts.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ~4.7 (5,000+ reviews). Praised for the lifting stones, the wreck history, and the lava-pinnacle approach path. Honest complaints: the wind is brutal and the lifting stones look smaller in person than in photos. Honest take: the most "story-rich" beach on Snæfellsnes — the combination of saga-style strength stones and a real shipwreck memorial gives it more depth than just black pebbles. Worth the stop.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. Park rangers occasionally on site in summer; not in April.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Sneaker waves — same hazard as Reynisfjara. Stay 25 m back.
  • Slippery rolly pebbles near the surf.
  • Lifting stones — lift with legs, do not drop on feet.
  • Do not touch or remove any wreck piece — protected memorial, prosecutable offence.
  • Severe wind exposure; check forecast.is.
