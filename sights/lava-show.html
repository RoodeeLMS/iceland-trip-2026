📖 Overview

The Icelandic Lava Show is a live indoor show in Vik (and a sister venue in Reykjavik) where staff melt real basalt to ~1100°C in a custom furnace and pour it into the show room directly in front of the audience. It is the only place in the world where you can experience flowing molten lava in a controlled, safe setting. The Vik venue is purpose-built and runs multiple shows daily. Booking ahead is essential — shows often sell out, especially in summer.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round — Multiple shows daily. Booking required via icelandiclavashow.com. Verify show times and book ahead the day before at minimum.

📷 Photography & Drone

Indoor low-light show. Bring a fast lens (35 mm f/1.8 or similar) and shoot at high ISO (3200-6400). Phone cameras handle the glow surprisingly well thanks to the bright lava itself. No flash — disrespectful to the audience and washes out the glow. 🚫 No drones — entirely indoor. The show staff also give clear photo guidance at the start.

🚗 Getting There

Central Vik village, walking distance from the petrol station and main shops. ~180 km from Reykjavik. Free parking on site. Any car.

  • Tickets: ฿1,900-2,400 adult depending on show type.
  • Booking required — book online before arriving in Vik.
  • Multiple shows daily — typically every 1-2 hours during opening hours.
  • Toilets and gift shop on site.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Indoor anchor for a rainy/windy afternoon in Vik, or simply a unique add-on to the Day 3 stop. 60-75 minutes total including arrival.

  • Best timing: Mid-afternoon shows leave you free for outdoor sights in the morning and evening. Rainy days: any time.
  • Crowds: Sells out frequently. Book ahead.
  • Combine with: Vik village stop (lunch, fuel), Vik church, Reynisfjara, Dyrholaey. The whole "Vik area" can fit in a half day with the Lava Show as the anchor.
  • Time budget: 75 minutes total.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.9 (4,000+ reviews) — one of the highest-rated paid attractions in Iceland. Universal praise for the uniqueness of seeing real molten lava up close. Common positive: "the only place in the world you can do this." Common complaint: high price (฿1,900-2,400) for a 25-minute show, and that it sells out.

Honest take: Worth the price if anyone in the group is interested in volcanic geology or wants a memorable indoor anchor. The price-per-minute is high but the experience is genuinely unique. If the day's outdoor weather is great and budget is tight, you can skip it and not miss a "must-do."

🎟️ Activities & Providers

The activity itself — book at icelandiclavashow.com. Classic show ฿1,900, Premium show (smaller group, longer experience) ฿2,400. Booking required.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Heat goggles are provided — wear them as instructed.
  • Stay seated and follow staff direction during the pour.
  • The heat blast at lava entry is intense but managed — staff brief the audience.
  • Not recommended for very young children or anyone with extreme heat sensitivity.
