📖 Overview

Foss á Síðu is a tall, thin waterfall that drops straight off a cliff face beside a small farmstead, visible directly from Ring Road 1 about 10 km east of Kirkjubæjarklaustur. It is a 5-minute photo stop — no detour, no walking, no fee. Worth the pull-off because the composition (waterfall + red-roofed farm + sometimes Icelandic horses) is one of the South Coast's most iconic Ring Road shots.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Always accessible. Visible from the Ring Road shoulder. No closures, no fees, no operator. In strong easterly winds the waterfall famously gets blown back upward over the cliff — a photogenic but real warning that wind here is fierce.

April 2026: Likely full flow from spring snowmelt. The pull-off shoulder may be muddy after rain.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Pull off on the Ring Road shoulderHeading east from Kirkjubæjarklaustur on Route 1, the waterfall appears on your left (north side) about 10 km out. There is a small unmarked gravel pull-off directly opposite the falls — slow well in advance, signal, and pull fully off the asphalt.
⏱️ 1 min · 📍 Route 1 shoulder, ~10 km E of Kirkjubæjarklaustur
2
Shoot from the shoulderDo NOT cross the fence or enter the farm — it is private property. The classic composition (waterfall, farm, cliff) works fine from the road shoulder with a 35-70mm lens. If horses are in the foreground field, that is the shot.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 At the pull-off
3
Pair with Dverghamrar across the roadThe Dverghamrar basalt columns (already on the sights list) are literally 200 m further east on the opposite side of the road. Walk or move the car — they pair as a single 15-minute combined stop.
⏱️ +10 min · 📍 200 m E, S side of Route 1

📷 Photography & Drone

Best angle: From the road shoulder, slightly east of the falls, including the red-roof farmhouse for scale. 35-70mm covers it. In strong winds time your shot for the gust — the spray blowing upward is the famous "reverse waterfall" effect documented in countless travel photos.

Best light: Morning — the cliff faces roughly south-east so the falls catch early light. By afternoon the cliff is in shadow.

🚫 Drone: Private farmland; do not fly over the property. From the road shoulder is fine in calm conditions but the wind here is among the worst on the South Coast — risk of losing the drone is high.

🚗 Getting There

  • Paved Ring Road shoulder. No parking lot, no walking.
  • ~10 km east of Kirkjubæjarklaustur on Route 1.
  • Free.
  • Any car, no 4x4.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Time needed: 5 min photo stop, or 15 min combined with Dverghamrar across the road.
  • Best time: Morning light on the cliff face.
  • Combine with: Dverghamrar (across the road), Kirkjubæjarklaustur village, Fjaðrárgljúfur canyon (15 min west).

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google ratings hover around 4.5. Visitors uniformly call it a "worth-the-stop drive-by" — nobody complains because expectations are correctly set. It is what it claims to be: a roadside waterfall you stop at for 5 minutes.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

The Ring Road has fast traffic; pull fully off the asphalt and watch for trucks before opening doors. Do not cross the fence onto private farmland.
