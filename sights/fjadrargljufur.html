Fjadrargljufur Canyon

📖 Overview

Fjadrargljufur is a 2 km long, up to 100 m deep serpentine canyon carved into palagonite tuff over the last 9,000 years by the Fjadra river, which still flows along the bottom. The walls are mossy, the bends are dramatic, and the rim trail offers a series of viewpoints. The canyon went viral in 2015 after Justin Bieber filmed a music video here, traffic exploded, the moss on the rim was trampled, and the site was closed for several months in 2019 for restoration. It has since reopened with a hardened, marked rim trail and clearly fenced viewpoints — the rules are now strictly enforced.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open as of April 2026, with the rim trail and viewpoints accessible. The bottom-of-canyon trail is closed in winter and shoulder seasons (typically reopens in summer when river levels drop and ice clears) — assume it is closed in early April. The rim trail is the visit; the bottom trail is summer-only.

April 2026 conditions: Rim trail may be muddy or have icy patches in spots. Stay on the marked path; the rim is fragile and falls into the canyon are fatal. Verify the access road is open — Route 206 is unpaved gravel and can be rough or briefly closed after heavy rain.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Drive Route 206 — gravel access roadTurn off Route 1 about 5 km east of Kirkjubaejarklaustur onto Route 206. ~2 km of gravel road; manageable in any 2WD car in dry conditions, slower-going if rutted. Free parking lot at the trailhead.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Route 206 trailhead lot
2
Read the trail rules at the startInformation board at the trailhead explains the closure history and the strict on-trail rule. Worth a 2-minute read so the rules feel earned, not arbitrary.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Trailhead board
3
First viewpoint — the dramatic upstream bendFrom the lot, walk up the gentle rim trail. The first major viewpoint is ~150 m in and looks down at the most dramatic upstream bend. Stay behind the wooden barriers — they're there because the rim has crumbled and people have died here.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 First viewpoint
4
Continue along the rim trailThe trail continues along the rim for ~1 km, with multiple fenced viewpoints along the way. Each gives a slightly different angle on the canyon's curves. Pace is gentle but uphill toward the head.
⏱️ 20-30 min · 📍 Rim trail
5
Top viewpoint at the head of the canyonAt the upper end of the trail, ~1 km from the lot, the final viewpoint looks back down the entire canyon — the postcard wide-angle. This is the shot to wait for.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Top viewpoint
6
Walk back to the lotSame trail back. Total round trip: 60-90 minutes. The bottom-of-canyon trail (the river-level walk through the canyon) is summer-only and closed in April.
⏱️ 20-30 min back · 📍 Return to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Top viewpoint, looking down the canyon: 16-24 mm wide. This is the iconic Fjadrargljufur shot — serpentine bends snaking away into the distance. Best in soft overcast or golden hour. Midday harsh sun creates ugly contrast.
  • First viewpoint, dramatic upstream bend: 24-35 mm. The closer angle shows the moss texture on the canyon walls and the river below.
  • Profile of the canyon walls: 50-85 mm from any viewpoint, frame the layered tuff against the moss for texture.
  • Best light: Soft overcast (very common in Iceland) brings out the moss greens. Golden hour is good but the deep canyon stays shadowed.
  • 🚫 Drone: Drones are explicitly prohibited at Fjadrargljufur per the Environment Agency of Iceland and on-site signage (post-Bieber, the rules tightened significantly). Do not fly here. See Planning page for general drone rules.

🚗 Getting There

Route 206, a 2 km gravel side road off Route 1, ~5 km east of Kirkjubaejarklaustur. Free parking at the trailhead. Any 2WD car in dry weather; check road conditions if recent heavy rain.

  • Parking: free.
  • No entry fee.
  • No facilities at the trailhead — use Kirkjubaejarklaustur 5 km west.
  • Walk to first viewpoint: ~150 m, gentle uphill.
  • Walk to top viewpoint: ~1 km, gentle uphill on a maintained trail.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Major Day 4 stop. 60-90 minutes. Pair with Eldhraun (~20 min west), Dverghamrar (~15 min west), and Kirkjubaejarklaustur (lunch/fuel, 5 min west).

  • Best light: Soft overcast or late afternoon. Avoid harsh midday sun.
  • Crowds: Moderate; far quieter than the south coast waterfalls thanks to the gravel access road.
  • Combine with: Eldhraun, Dverghamrar, Hofskirkja (farther east), Skaftafell.
  • Time budget: 75 min for the full rim trail round trip.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.7. Reviews are overwhelmingly positive on the canyon itself and the post-restoration trail design. Common complaints: occasional closure for further restoration, gravel access road, and the bottom trail being closed most of the year. Some visitors are disappointed they can't walk down into the canyon.

Honest take: Lives up to the hype if you set expectations correctly. The rim trail is the experience; the bottom-of-canyon trail (the original viral attraction) is realistically not available outside summer. Even just from above, the canyon is one of the most photogenic landscapes on the south coast.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Stay on the marked trail and behind barriers. The rim has crumbled in places and falls into the canyon are fatal — there have been deaths here from people stepping off the path for photos.
  • Trail can be muddy or icy in spring; non-slip footwear helps.
  • Wind on the rim can be strong — brace tripods and small children.
  • Bottom-of-canyon trail is closed in winter and shoulder seasons; do not attempt to descend.
