📖 Overview

Kerið is a 3,000-year-old volcanic crater on the Tjarnarhólar row, with vivid red-orange scoria walls and a turquoise-blue lake at the bottom. Unlike most Icelandic volcanic features it's compact, photogenic, and accessible — a flat 10-minute loop walks the entire rim. The site is privately owned by Arctic Adventures (purchased 2023) and charges a small admission fee, which is why it's not on the official "free" Golden Circle list but has become a near-mandatory final stop because it's directly on Route 35 between Selfoss and Reykjavik.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round — Daylight hours only. April hours roughly 09:00-19:00. Per kerid.is (official).

April 2026 conditions: The lake is often still frozen or partially iced over in early April, with snow on the rim path. The contrast of red walls + white snow + blue ice is its own kind of stunning, just different from the summer turquoise. Do NOT walk on the frozen lake — see safety section.

Admission: ISK 700 ≈ ฿175 per adult (children 12 and under free). Card/cash at the small kiosk by the lot.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Pull off Route 35, park, pay at the kiosk Kerid is signposted on the east side of Route 35, ~15 km north of Selfoss. The lot is gravel, free, with toilets. Pay ฿175 per adult at the small wooden kiosk on the way to the rim — card accepted. Children under 12 free.
⏱️ 3 min · 📍 Kerid lot, beside Route 35
2
First view from the rim — the high overlook From the kiosk, walk ~50 m up a gentle slope to the first rim viewpoint. This is the highest and best top-down angle into the crater — red scoria walls dropping ~55 m to the lake/ice. Stop here for the wide-angle shot before walking the loop.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Rim overlook, ~50m from kiosk
3
Walk the rim loop clockwise A clear, flat ~400 m gravel path follows the entire rim of the crater. Going clockwise, the path drops gently around the eastern side. Each step gives a slightly different angle on the red walls and the lake centre. In April, watch for icy patches on the shaded north side. Do not step off the path — the scoria is loose and the slopes are steep.
⏱️ 10-15 min · 📍 Rim loop, ~400 m total
4
Descend to the lake on the wooden staircase (lower path) On the southern side of the rim a wooden staircase leads down to a small viewing area at lake level. This is the only sanctioned route to the bottom — and the only place you can see the crater walls towering above you. Total descent ~55 m, ~80 steps. Very icy in April; use the handrail. Do not step out onto the frozen lake — the ice is unsafe.
⏱️ 10-15 min round-trip · 📍 South-side wooden staircase
5
Return up the staircase, finish the rim loop, back to the lot Climb back to the rim and complete the remaining ~150 m of the loop back to the kiosk and lot. Total time on site is about 30-40 minutes. From here it's ~45 min back to Reykjavik on Route 35 → Route 1.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Loop completion

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Wide rim shot: 16-24 mm from the highest north rim viewpoint captures the entire crater bowl. Shoot mid-day when the sun lights both walls.
  • Telephoto detail of the red walls: 70-200 mm picks out the texture of the scoria — the saturated red is the photographic point of this stop.
  • From the lake floor looking up: Wide-angle straight up the wall with a person at the base for scale.
  • Best light: 11:00-14:00 — the sun needs to be high enough to reach the bottom of the bowl. Early morning leaves the south wall in deep shadow.

🚫 Drone: Drone flying at Kerid is prohibited by the site owner (Arctic Adventures). Signage at the kiosk states no drones. The crater is also a popular spot with crowds and the bowl creates unpredictable wind eddies. Don't fly here. General Iceland drone rules: Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 35, immediately east of the road. Any 2WD rental car. Very hard to miss — large brown sign and gravel lot.

  • From Gullfoss: ~50 min south via Route 35 → 359 → 35.
  • From Reykjavik direct: ~50 min / 60 km via Route 1 → 35.
  • From Fridheimar: ~25 min south on Route 35.
  • Parking: Free gravel lot beside Route 35.
  • Entry fee: ISK 700 ≈ ฿175 per adult; under-12s free.
  • Walk from lot to first rim view: ~50 m, gentle slope.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Quick stop, ~30-40 minutes total. Best treated as the final Golden Circle stop on the way back to Reykjavik — its position on Route 35 makes it a zero-detour add-on after Gullfoss/Geysir.

  • Best light: Mid-day sun (11:00-14:00) lights the bowl evenly. Avoid first/last hour of daylight when half the crater is in shadow.
  • Crowds: Small lot, can fill up at peak (12:00-15:00). Quieter after 16:00.
  • Combine with: Order it after Gullfoss/Geysir/Fridheimar so it's the last stop before driving back to Reykjavik. Don't backtrack for it.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google: ~4.5 stars across 15,000+ reviews. Tripadvisor: solidly positive with the main complaint being the entry fee — some reviewers consider it "tourist tax" since the site is otherwise just a 30-min walk. The defenders point out that the path maintenance, kiosk, toilets and parking are all paid for from the fee.

Honest take: Slightly overrated as a "must-see" but genuinely worth the ฿175 IF you're already passing it on Route 35. Don't make a special trip. The crater is photogenic and unique-looking but the visit is short and the fee feels marginally pricey for what you get. Skip only if running out of daylight.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. The site is owned and operated by Arctic Adventures; they sometimes pre-sell entry tickets online but it's pointless given there's never a queue at the kiosk.

⚠️ Safety Notes

Per safetravel.is active pin at this location:

  • "Ice on the lake is unsafe — do not walk on it." The lake is shallow and the ice is unpredictable; falling through into ~2°C water is a serious cold-water immersion event.
  • Stay on marked paths — the scoria slopes are loose and steep. Slipping off the rim or staircase is the main injury risk.
  • Icy staircase in April — use the handrail, take it slow.
  • No barriers on most of the rim — keep children close and away from the edge.
  • No drone flying per site policy.
