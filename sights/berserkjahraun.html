Berserkjahraun Lava Field

Day 8
📍 Coordinates64.9861, -22.8878
📅 Trip DayDay 8
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions▶️ Watch on YouTube

📖 Overview

Berserkjahraun is a ~4,000-year-old lava field on the north side of Snæfellsnes, between Stykkishólmur and Grundarfjörður, named after a saga in which two Swedish berserker warriors were given the impossible task of clearing a path across the lava. The field is best experienced as a scenic drive-through on Route 558 (a short gravel cut-through between Routes 54 and 56) rather than as a hike: you wind between mossy lava ridges, small craters, and a couple of optional 4x4-only side roads. It is more about the atmosphere and a couple of pull-off photo stops than a "destination."

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round. Route 558 is gravel but normally fine for any rental car in dry conditions; can be soft after heavy rain. Some side branches are F-roads (4x4 only).

April 2026: Gravel road may have residual snow patches and soft spots. Drive slowly and check conditions on road.is. Side F-roads (e.g. F570 area) are closed in April.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Turn off Route 54 onto Route 558 (gravel)From the Kirkjufell / Grundarfjörður direction, head ~25 km east on Route 54 to the signed junction with Route 558. The gravel cut-through is ~12 km long and connects to Route 56 near Hraunsfjörður. Drive slowly: 30-50 km/h.
⏱️ 1 min · 📍 Junction with Rt 54
2
Pull off at the first viewpoint over the lava~2 km in, the road climbs over a small rise with a wide pull-off on the right. Step out for a wide view over the moss-covered lava ridges with Hraunsfjörður bay behind. Photo + 5 min.
⏱️ 5-10 min · 📍 ~2 km in
3
Walk a short loop on the lava (5 min)From the pull-off, an informal track lets you walk ~50 m onto the moss-covered lava for texture. Do not step on the moss — stick to the bare rock track. The 4,000-year-old moss cushions are extremely slow to recover.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Lava edge
4
Continue the drive — small cratersFurther along the road passes between several small scoria cones (Rauðhálsar / Rauðukúlur). Slow down for them; one has a small unmarked pull-off where you can step out and look up at the red cone.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Mid-route
5
Connect to Route 56 — exitThe gravel road meets paved Route 56 near the head of Hraunsfjörður. From here you can continue south to the Vatnaleið junction (toward Selvallafoss) or back north to Stykkishólmur.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Rt 56 exit

📷 Photography & Drone

  • Wide landscape: 16-35 mm from the first pull-off — moss texture foreground, fjord background.
  • Tight texture: 50-100 mm of moss cushions on lava — graphic abstract, best in soft light.
  • Crater portrait: 35 mm of one of the Rauðhálsar red scoria cones against blue sky.
  • Best light: Soft overcast for moss colour saturation; harsh midday sun washes the green out.

🚁 Drone: Outside the national park here, no formal local ban. The lava moss is environmentally fragile but drones do not affect it from the air. Standard courtesy: keep clear of any other visitors on the road. See Planning drone section.

🚗 Getting There

Gravel Route 558 (12 km cut-through between Rt 54 and Rt 56). Any rental car in normal conditions. Side branches are F-roads — closed in April and 4x4-only when open.

  • Parking: Informal pull-offs along the gravel road.
  • Walk: None required — this is a drive-through. Optional 5 min walks at pull-offs.
  • Fees: None.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 30-45 min for the drive-through with 2-3 short photo stops. Up to 60 min if you linger.
  • Best light: Soft / overcast for moss colour. Late afternoon side-light on the cones.
  • Combine with: Stykkishólmur (15 min north), Kirkjufell (30 min west on Route 54), Selvallafoss (15 min south on Route 56). All Day 8.
  • Skip if: Heavy rain (gravel turns soft) or you are short on time. Berserkjahraun is the lowest-priority of the Day 8 stops.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ratings are sparse — most visitors rate the road, not a destination. Consistent feedback: "beautiful drive, glad we took it instead of staying on Route 54." Honest take: not a "stop" so much as a scenic alternative routing. Worth the extra 15-20 minutes vs the main road if you have time and dry conditions; skip if rushed or wet.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Gravel road — drive slowly, watch for soft spots and potholes.
  • Stay on bare rock; do not step on the moss.
  • F-road side branches are closed and signposted; do not enter with a 2WD.
  • Limited mobile signal in the lava field.
