📖 Overview

Thingvellir is the most historically and geologically significant site in Iceland — both the founding location of the Althing (the world's oldest surviving parliament, established in 930 AD) and the only place on land where you can walk inside the rift between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates. UNESCO inscribed it as a World Heritage Site in 2004 for its cultural value, and it's a national park in its own right. For most Golden Circle visitors it's the first stop of the day and often the most rewarding — a single short walk delivers a parliament site, a waterfall, and a continental rift in under an hour.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round — Visitor centre at Hakid is open daily; park trails are open with no major closures reported for April 2026.

April 2026 conditions: Snow and ice patches still likely on the Almannagja boardwalk, especially the descending ramp from Hakid and shaded sections near Öxarárfoss. Bring micro-spikes or be ready to step carefully — falls on the iced-over wooden ramps are the single most common complaint on Tripadvisor for this season. The Öxará river runs high with snowmelt so the falls are loud and full.

Daylight in early April is ~14 hours, so timing is flexible. The visitor centre opens at 09:00. No seasonal trail closures inside the rift area itself.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at P1 Hakid — the upper visitor centre lot Coming from Reykjavik on Route 36, P1 Hakid is signposted on the right and sits on the western escarpment directly above the rift. Pay parking via the Parka app: ฿250 (ISK 1,000) covers all park lots P1-P5 for the entire day. Skip the smaller lots — Hakid puts you at the top of the rift descent and saves backtracking. Use the toilets here; there are none down in the rift.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 P1 Hakid lot, 64.2581, -21.1303
2
Hakid viewpoint platform — read the rift from above Walk 60 m from the lot to the wooden viewing deck at the lip of Almannagja. This is the single best orientation shot of the park: the rift cuts left-right beneath you, the Lögberg (Law Rock) site is visible to the east, and Lake Thingvallavatn fills the horizon. Read the interpretive panels — they explain that you are standing on the North American plate looking across at the Eurasian plate 7 km away.
⏱️ 5-10 min · 📍 Hakid platform, 60m from P1
3
Descend into Almannagja — walk between the plates Take the wooden ramp/stairs down into the gorge. For the next ~700 m you're walking along the floor of the Almannagja rift with sheer basalt cliffs (the eastern edge of the North American plate) rising 30-40 m on your left. This is the literal "mid-Atlantic ridge on dry land" experience. Watch for ice on the ramp in April. Stop at the marked Lögberg (Law Rock) site about a third of the way down — a flagpole marks where the chieftains gave speeches at the Althing for ~800 years.
⏱️ 20-25 min · 📍 Almannagja rift floor, 0-700m from Hakid
4
Detour up to Öxarárfoss waterfall Roughly 500 m down the rift, a signed wooden boardwalk branches left (west) up a short slope. Follow it 200 m to Öxarárfoss, where the Öxará river drops ~20 m over the rift wall into a pool. In April it's running hard with snowmelt and the basalt is dark and wet — high contrast, very photogenic. The boardwalk gets icy; grip the rail. Return the same way to the main rift path.
⏱️ 15-20 min · 📍 Öxarárfoss, ~500m down rift then 200m west
5
Continue to Thingvellir Church and the farmhouses Keep walking down the rift until it opens out into the plain. Cross the small wooden bridge over the Öxará river and you'll see the white-and-black Thingvellirkirkja (1859) and the five interconnected gabled buildings of the Prime Minister's summer residence. The church and graveyard are free to enter — the interior is plain Icelandic Lutheran with a small altar painting. Skáldareitur (the Poets' Cemetery) next to it holds the graves of Jónas Hallgrímsson and Einar Benediktsson.
⏱️ 10-15 min · 📍 Thingvellirkirkja, ~1 km from Hakid
6
Peer into Peningagja and Flosagja fissures From the church area, walk ~200 m east along the trail to find Peningagja ("coin fissure"), a narrow water-filled crack where visitors traditionally throw coins. The water is glass-clear blue from filtered glacier melt — same source water as Silfra. Adjacent Flosagja is the larger fissure system. Don't enter the water (it's ~2°C and protected); just look down. Brilliant aerial-style photo straight down into blue.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Peningagja/Flosagja fissures, ~200m east of church
7
Skip Silfra (we don't cold-water swim) — note it for context Silfra is the famous fissure where divers and snorkelers swim in 2°C glacial water between the tectonic plates. Booked tours run ฿7,000-12,000 per person via Dive.is and Arctic Adventures. Our group doesn't swim in cold water — skip. You can still walk past the Silfra parking area (P5) and look down into the crystal-clear entry pool from the rim, which gives a sense of the visibility for free.
⏱️ 5 min (optional walk-by) · 📍 P5 Silfra rim, ~700m further east
8
Loop back to Hakid via the upper trail (or shuttle road) Either retrace the rift back to P1 (more atmospheric, ~25 min) or take the gravel service road that climbs the western slope back to the visitor centre (less scenic but ice-free). At Hakid, the Visitor Centre has a free 10-minute film on the geology and parliament history that's genuinely worth the time — much more substantive than the typical tourist exhibit. Then drive on to Bruarfoss / Geysir.
⏱️ 25 min walk + 15 min film · 📍 Back to P1 Hakid Visitor Centre

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Hakid platform, wide: 16-24 mm to capture both the rift dropping away and the lake on the horizon. Best in morning when the eastern light hits the western cliff face.
  • Almannagja from below: Stand on the rift floor, point the wide lens straight up the cliff face. Person in frame for scale.
  • Öxarárfoss: 24-50 mm framed tight on the falls and the dark basalt amphitheatre. Slow shutter (0.5-1 sec) on a tripod for silky water — but the boardwalk vibrates so brace against the rail.
  • Peningagja: Shoot straight down with a polariser on to cut surface reflection — the blue is unreal.

🚫 Drone: PERMIT REQUIRED — effectively prohibited for casual visitors. Per the official Thingvellir National Park drone policy, personal drone flying is restricted to early morning (before 09:00) or late afternoon (after 18:00), must avoid all popular sites (Hak, Almannagja, Lögberg, Flosagja, Öxarárfoss, the church, Silfra), and a written permission from the park is required for filming. In practice: do not fly here during a normal Golden Circle visit. Park rangers actively enforce. Save the drone for the rental car day on Snaefellsnes.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 36 from Reykjavik — 45 km, ~50 minutes. Any rental car. The road is well-maintained and ploughed in winter.

  • P1 Hakid (recommended): top of Almannagja, next to Visitor Centre. 64.2581, -21.1303.
  • P2 / P5: alternative lower lots near the church and Silfra — only useful if P1 is full or you want to start from the church.
  • Parking fee: ISK 1,000 ≈ ฿250 per car per day, valid in all park lots. Pay via the Parka app or at the kiosk. No on-site cash machine.
  • No park entry fee — only parking is charged.
  • Toilets at Hakid Visitor Centre (free) and at P5 Silfra. None on the rift trail itself.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

★ Star sight — the most important stop on Day 2 and worth real time. Allocate 1.5-2 hours minimum on the ground, 2.5 hours if you watch the visitor centre film.

  • Best time of day: First stop after leaving Reykjavik — arrive 09:00-09:30 to beat the 11:00 tour bus wave. Morning light also rakes the western rift wall well.
  • Crowds: Heaviest 11:00-15:00 from Reykjavik day-tour buses. By 16:00 it's quiet again, but you'll want to be at Geysir/Gullfoss by then.
  • Combine with (in order): Thingvellir → Bruarfoss (40 min drive) → Fridheimar lunch (15 min) → Geysir (10 min) → Gullfoss (10 min) → Kerid (50 min, on the way back to Reykjavik).
  • Don't try to "do" Thingvellir in 30 minutes — it's the one Golden Circle stop where rushing genuinely diminishes the experience.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps: ~4.7 stars across 50,000+ reviews. Tripadvisor: #1 thing to do in Selfoss/southern Iceland region. Universally praised for the Almannagja walk, the historical weight (people are visibly moved at the Lögberg site), and the sheer geological clarity — you can actually see the plate boundary, not just imagine it.

Honest take: Not overrated. The single most common complaint is parking fees and slippery boardwalks in winter — both addressable with the Parka app and decent footwear. The visitor centre film is unusually good. Skip it only if you've seen an extensive geology documentary on Iceland already. The Silfra snorkel is not the only reason to come — the rift walk alone justifies the stop.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided is the right move for our group. The walking is the main event and no booking is needed. Optional add-ons (skipped on this trip but listed for completeness):

  • Silfra snorkel via Dive.is or Arctic Adventures — ~฿7,000-9,000 (snorkel) / ฿15,000-20,000 (dive). Drysuit provided. Skipped — group doesn't cold-water swim.
  • Visitor Centre film — free with parking. Highly recommended.
  • Ranger-guided walks — occasional in summer only, not running in April.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Ice on boardwalks (April): The wooden ramps from Hakid down into Almannagja and the boardwalk to Öxarárfoss are the highest-risk fall hazards. Micro-spikes or YakTrax are smart in early April.
  • Stay on marked trails — fissures here are deep and some are partially overgrown with moss. Stepping off-trail near Flosagja is genuinely dangerous.
  • Do not enter the water at Peningagja or Flosagja — protected, and the cold (~2°C) will incapacitate quickly.
  • No drone without permit — fines apply. See Photography section.
