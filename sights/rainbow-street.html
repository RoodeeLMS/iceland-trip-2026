Rainbow Street (Skolavordustigur)

Day 1
📍 Coordinates64.1425, -21.9275
📅 Trip DayDay 1
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Skolavordustigur is the pedestrianised upper block of a side street that runs from Laugavegur to the front of Hallgrimskirkja. About 200 m of it is painted in rainbow stripes — originally for Reykjavik Pride 2015, made permanent in 2019. It's one of Reykjavik's three or four most-photographed spots, and it's free, always open, and 30 seconds from the church.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Permanent installation, always accessible. The paint is repainted by the city annually (residents and visitors can volunteer with brushes during Pride week per Your Friend in Reykjavik). The colours can look a bit faded by the end of winter — by April 2026 expect slightly worn paint, not fresh.

April 2026 conditions: The street is pedestrianised so no cars to dodge. Surface can be wet/icy in early April mornings — wear shoes with grip. Daylight is plentiful so no rush.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Start at the bottom of Skolavordustigur (Laugavegur end) From Laugavegur, turn south onto Skolavordustigur. The painted section starts a short way up. Stand at the lower end of the rainbow stripes and look uphill — Hallgrimskirkja's tower frames perfectly at the top of the street. This is the iconic shot.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Bottom of pedestrianised section
2
Walk slowly up — browse the design shops The street is lined with the best of Reykjavik's small design economy: the Handknitting Association of Iceland (real lopapeysa, not gift-shop knockoffs), Geysir clothing, Kron and Aurum jewellery, Mokka-Kaffi (a 1958 cafe famous for its waffles). You'll naturally stop a few times — that's the point.
⏱️ 20-40 min · 📍 200m painted stretch
3
Get the centred shot from the middle of the street Halfway up, crouch low in the centre of the painted lane and shoot uphill. Low angle exaggerates the leading lines and the rainbow colours dominate the frame with the church spire dead-centre. The street is pedestrianised so you can take your time, but step aside if other photographers are queueing.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Mid-street, ~100m up
4
Top of the street — you're at Hallgrimskirkja The paint ends right at the church plaza. Continue straight into the Hallgrimskirkja visit (Leif statue → tower → nave). The two stops naturally combine into one.
⏱️ Continues into Hallgrimskirkja visit

📷 Photography & Drone

  • Lead-line shot: 24-35 mm, low angle, centre of the street, pointing uphill at the church. The classic.
  • Best time: Morning 9:00-11:00 (sun lights the front of the church and the colours pop) or after a light rain when the wet paint saturates dramatically.
  • Crowds: Before 10:00 the street is nearly empty; by midday there are usually 10-20 people clustered taking the same shot. Be patient or shoot early.
  • People-free shots: Wait for a gap, or use a long exposure (1-2 sec) with an ND filter to blur passers-by.

🚫 Drone: No. Same as Hallgrimskirkja — central Reykjavik is BIRK controlled airspace and this is a busy public space (Icelandic Transport Authority). General drone rules on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

  • Walk: Right off Laugavegur. From any downtown hotel, 5-15 min walk.
  • Parking: P2 zone metered street parking on the surrounding blocks (~ISK 220/hr ≈ ฿55). Pedestrianised section is closed to cars. Free Sundays.
  • Fees: None. Open 24/7.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Always combine with Hallgrimskirkja — they are literally the same stop.
  • Best light: Morning (sun on the church facade) or just after rain.
  • Allocate: 15-30 minutes including photos and a quick browse of one shop. If you want to actually shop, budget 60+ min.
  • Combine with: Hallgrimskirkja (top), Mokka-Kaffi for waffles, then drop down to Laugavegur for lunch.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Tripadvisor and Google reviews of Skolavordustigur skew 4.5+ — visitors enjoy the photo opportunity and the shopping. It's not a destination on its own; it's an enjoyable 200 m walk to the church.

Honest take: Take the shot, browse one shop, move on. Anyone padding "Rainbow Street" into a 1-hour standalone activity is overdoing it. As part of the Hallgrimskirkja visit it's perfect — pleasant, free, photogenic, ten minutes.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided. No tickets, no tours.

⚠️ Safety Notes

None specific. Painted surface can be slippery when wet or icy — watch your footing in April mornings.
