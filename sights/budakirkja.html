Búðakirkja Black Church

Day 7
📍 Coordinates64.8214, -23.3819
📅 Trip DayDay 7
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Búðakirkja is a tiny black-tarred wooden church standing alone on the Búðahraun lava field, with Snæfellsjökull glacier visible to the west on a clear day. Built in 1703 and rebuilt in 1848 and 1987, it is one of the most photographed churches in Iceland — not because of its architecture but because of the stark contrast of black timber against green moss, white snow, or the open Atlantic horizon. The visit is short and entirely about the photo and the setting.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open / accessible year-round. Free. The interior is normally locked outside service times — assume you will see the exterior only.

April 2026: No closures expected. The surrounding lava field can be patchy with snow and wet moss; stick to the gravel path. The graveyard is active and private — be respectful, no climbing on stones or crosses.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the Hotel Búðir lotTurn off Route 574 onto the short signed track to Hotel Búðir. A free gravel lot serves both the hotel and the church. Walk past the hotel toward the obvious black structure ~150 m south.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Hotel Búðir lot
2
Approach via the gravel path, not across the mossA roped gravel path leads from the lot directly to the church gate. The surrounding 4,000-year-old Búðahraun moss takes decades to recover from a single boot print — do not shortcut across it. Photograph the church from the path approach: the side-on angle from the north-east is the most-shot composition.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Path to church
3
Walk the perimeter of the graveyardFollow the low stone wall fully around the church and graveyard (clockwise is easiest). Each side gives a different background: north = lava field + Snæfellsjökull, east = open Atlantic, south = the wooden gate with iron arch ("1703" date stone), west = the hotel and grass slopes. Three minutes per side.
⏱️ 12 min · 📍 Around the church
4
Frame the iconic gate-and-arch shotThe black wrought-iron arch over the south gate, with the church behind it and the glacier on the horizon, is the signature composition. Stand 3-4 m back from the gate, kneel slightly, use a wide-to-normal lens.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 South gate
5
Optional: 5-minute walk into the lava fieldFrom the church, a faint track heads west across the moss for 200 m to a small rise that gives a wider environment shot of the church inside the full lava-and-glacier landscape. Stay on the track.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 200 m west

📷 Photography & Drone

  • Signature shot: South gate with iron arch, church behind, glacier on horizon. 24-35 mm wide, f/8, kneel slightly so the arch frames the church door.
  • Side profile: North-east angle from the path, 35-50 mm, captures the black timber against pale lava.
  • Best light: Overcast or soft late-afternoon light flatters the black timber more than midday sun. Sunset behind the glacier (west) makes a strong silhouette.
  • Aurora: Dark, low horizon, no light pollution — excellent aurora foreground if you are passing at night.

🚫 Drone: Do not fly directly over the church or graveyard — it is a consecrated active burial site and the hotel asks visitors to respect privacy. Standing-altitude photography only on the church side. For general drone rules see Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 574 (Snæfellsnesvegur), then a 200 m gravel access road to Hotel Búðir. Any rental car. ~10 min west of Vegamót, ~15 min west of Ytri-Tunga.

  • Parking: Free, at Hotel Búðir.
  • Walk: ~150 m flat gravel from lot to church gate.
  • Fees: None.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 20-30 min. It is a photo stop, not a sight you "tour."
  • Best light: Overcast, golden hour, or blue hour. Avoid harsh midday backlight from the south.
  • Crowds: Even in summer manageable; in April you will likely have it to yourselves.
  • Combine with: Ytri-Tunga (15 min east) and Arnarstapi (25 min west) on the Day 7 loop.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ~4.7 (4,000+ reviews). Universally praised for the photogenic setting; the few complaints are about the church being locked (it almost always is) or about other visitors trampling the moss. Honest take: exactly as advertised — a small, beautiful, quick photo stop. Not a "destination" on its own but mandatory if you are passing.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. Hotel Búðir next door has a restaurant if you need a coffee stop.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • The lava moss is fragile — stay on the gravel path.
  • Active graveyard — do not climb stones, do not enter the church if a service is in progress.
  • Strong coastal wind; black timber gets slippery when wet.
