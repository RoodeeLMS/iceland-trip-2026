Saxhóll Crater

Day 7
📍 Coordinates64.8875, -23.9014
📅 Trip DayDay 7
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Saxhóll is a 109 m volcanic scoria cone on the western tip of Snæfellsnes, just inside Snæfellsjökull National Park. A spiral metal staircase built into the cone's flank lets you walk to the rim in ~10 minutes for a 360° view over the lava fields, the Atlantic, and the glacier itself. It is the easiest crater summit on the entire Snæfellsnes peninsula — a 20-30 minute total stop with disproportionately good payoff.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free. Metal staircase is well maintained but can be icy in winter and shoulder season.

April 2026: Steps may have residual snow/ice — be careful, especially the upper section. Drone permit required (national park).

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the Saxhóll lotFree gravel lot directly off Route 574, signposted "Saxhóll." ~15 spaces. The cone is right next to the lot — you cannot miss it.
⏱️ 1 min · 📍 64.8875, -23.9014
2
Climb the metal staircaseA galvanised steel staircase spirals up the south flank of the cone. ~400 steps, gentle gradient, hand rail on one side. About 8-12 minutes up at a relaxed pace, less if fit. Stop at the landings to catch breath and take photos.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 The cone
3
Walk the rimAt the top, a narrow path traces the full circular rim. Walking the entire rim (~5 min) gives you every angle: south to the Atlantic and Lóndrangar, east to Snæfellsjökull glacier, west to the open ocean and the offshore sea stacks of Skarðsvík, north to the lava fields stretching to Ólafsvík.
⏱️ 5-10 min · 📍 Crater rim
4
Look down into the craterThe bowl is small, dry, and red-ochre, with no lake or steam — just classic scoria cone interior. 30 seconds is enough.
⏱️ 1 min · 📍 Rim
5
Walk back downSame staircase. ~5 minutes down. Watch your footing on icy steps in April.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Back to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

  • From the rim, looking south-east: 24-35 mm wide capturing Snæfellsjökull glacier on the horizon with the lava field foreground.
  • The staircase itself: Top-down 24 mm of the spiral steps with a person climbing — graphic abstract shot.
  • Looking west: Late afternoon, ocean horizon, open Atlantic light.
  • Best light: Late afternoon for the glacier; any time for the staircase abstract.

🚁 Drone — PERMIT REQUIRED: Inside Snæfellsjökull National Park. Permit required from park warden. See Planning drone section.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 574, free gravel lot directly at the cone. Any rental car. ~15 min north of Lóndrangar.

  • Parking: Free, ~15 spaces.
  • Walk: ~10 min up steel staircase, ~5 min down.
  • Fees: None.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 20-30 min total.
  • Best light: Any. Late afternoon for warm light on the glacier east of you.
  • Combine with: Lóndrangar (15 min south) and Djúpalónssandur (20 min south) — natural Day 7 western tip trio.
  • Skip if: Heavy ice on the staircase or strong gusts at the rim.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ~4.6 (3,000+ reviews). Praised as the highest payoff for the smallest effort on Snæfellsnes — "10 minutes up, top of a volcano." Complaints are limited to "I expected something more dramatic at the top" (it is a small dry bowl, not Krafla). Honest take: a guaranteed quick win on the loop. Always include unless icy.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Metal staircase can be icy/slippery in shoulder season — use the handrail.
  • Strong winds at the rim — watch children, do not lean over the edge.
  • No fall protection on the rim path — stay on the marked trace.
