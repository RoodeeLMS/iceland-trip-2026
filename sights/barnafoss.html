Barnafoss (Children's Falls)

Day 6
📍 Coordinates64.7028, -20.9756
📅 Trip DayDay 6 (West / Silver Circle)
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Barnafoss — "Children's Falls" — sits ~300 m upstream of Hraunfossar on the same Hvita river. Where Hraunfossar is wide and gentle, Barnafoss is the opposite: a violent, churning blue-white rapid forced through a narrow basalt cleft. Its name comes from a folk legend: two children from a nearby farm were said to have crossed a natural stone arch over the falls one Christmas and drowned. The grieving mother had the arch destroyed (some accounts say by curse) so no one would die there again. Visit it as the second half of the Hraunfossar stop — same parking lot, 5 minute walk.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free, no closures — Path and footbridge from Hraunfossar are accessible all year. No active SafeTravel alert.

April 2026 conditions: Spring melt = peak flow and the falls look most dramatic. Spray on the footbridge can freeze overnight; the metal grating gets slick — micro-spikes recommended in early morning. The path from Hraunfossar is short and well-marked; no winter route closure has been reported.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Start from the Hraunfossar parking lot Same lot, same restrooms (1,000 ISK / ~฿250 cash). If you've already done Hraunfossar, just continue east on the marked path along the river. Total walking from car to Barnafoss is ~5 min one way.
⏱️ 0 min · 📍 Shared lot, off Rd 518
2
Cross the footbridge over the Hvita gorge The metal-and-wood bridge spans the river between the two falls. Mid-bridge is the only spot you can look directly down into the narrow rapid below. Hold the rail — spray from peak spring flow makes the grating slick.
⏱️ 3 min · 📍 Footbridge between Hraunfossar & Barnafoss
3
Main viewpoint at the head of the rapid A short path leads to a fenced platform right above the narrow basalt cleft where the river compresses and explodes. This is where the natural stone arch is said to have stood. The blue-white colour and the violence of the water make it photographically very different from the gentle Hraunfossar curtain a minute back the way you came.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Barnafoss main platform
4
Walk back via the upstream side On the return, take the slightly higher path to see the Hraunfossar lava cliff from a different angle. Read the Barnafoss legend interpretive sign at the trailhead before you leave — the story is genuinely moody and the kind of context the photos don't carry on their own.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Return path to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Top-down from the platform: 24-50 mm to fit the full curve of the rapid in frame. 1/250 sec or faster freezes the white-water texture; 1/4 sec turns it to silk — shoot both.
  • From the footbridge looking upstream: Wide angle 16-24 mm captures the cleft and the basalt walls framing it.
  • Best light: Overcast strongly preferred — direct sun blows out the white water and ruins the blue-green colour. April's typically grey sky here is an asset.
  • Pair with Hraunfossar: Same visit, totally different visual style — use them as a contrast pair in the trip album.

🚁 Drone: No site-specific ban. Same notes as Hraunfossar — wind funnels through the gorge, launch from the parking-lot side, mind any visitors on the platforms. For general Iceland drone law see the Drone section on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Same parking lot as Hraunfossar (off Route 518). Free, paved, 5 min flat walk to Barnafoss along marked path.

  • Parking: Free, shared with Hraunfossar.
  • Restrooms: 1,000 ISK ≈ ฿250, cash only.
  • Walk: ~300 m one way, easy gravel path with footbridge.
  • Entry: Free.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Always combined with Hraunfossar — they share a lot and trail. Combined time: ~45 min for both. Don't try to "skip" Barnafoss to save time; it's literally on the path between the lot and the second half of the Hraunfossar viewpoints.

  • Best light: Overcast or early morning. Avoid harsh midday sun.
  • Crowds: Even quieter than Hraunfossar — most visitors stop at the main deck and turn around. April will be empty.
  • Combine with: Hraunfossar (mandatory), Deildartunguhver hot spring (~30 km south).
  • Allocate ~15 min for the Barnafoss portion after the main Hraunfossar viewing.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Tripadvisor rating ~4.5/5. Visitors usually describe it as the "darker, angrier" companion to Hraunfossar, and value it most for the legend and the contrasting visual character. Common note: photos undersell how loud and physical it is in person.

Honest take: On its own, not a destination — just another short basalt rapid. As the second half of the Hraunfossar stop it's worth every minute. The legend genuinely changes how the spot reads, so read the sign before you stare.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • The platforms are well-fenced, but the path from the footbridge can be slick from spray and ice in April — micro-spikes recommended.
  • Do not step over the railing. The current is fatal and there is no recovery.
  • Hold children's hands at the footbridge and the main platform. The legend is not a metaphor — the river really did kill two kids here.
  • No active SafeTravel alert at time of research.
