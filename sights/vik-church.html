📖 Overview

Vikurkirkja is the small white-and-red Lutheran church on the hill above Vik village, completed in 1934. It's photogenic on its own but the main reason to stop is the view from the hilltop: a clean panorama of Vik village below, the black-sand beach, and the Reynisdrangar sea stacks rising offshore. It is also the designated emergency evacuation point for Vik in the event of a Katla eruption — the only structure expected to survive the resulting flood.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open — Church grounds are accessible year-round at no cost. Interior is open for services and occasional visitor hours; not guaranteed during a casual stop.

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Church + Reynisdrangar in one frame: 35-50 mm from the southwest corner of the lot — frames the church's red roof in the foreground with the sea stacks beyond. Iconic Vik composition.
  • Wide panorama from the south: 24 mm of village + beach + stacks. Best in late afternoon when light is on the sea stacks.
  • Sunset: The west-facing position means the church is side-lit at sunset and the sea stacks are bathed in warm light.
  • 🚫 Drone: Active village/church location — not appropriate. Skip drones here.

🚗 Getting There

Direct uphill from the center of Vik village on a short paved road. Small free lot at the church. Any car. ~1 minute drive from Route 1.

  • Parking: free.
  • No entry fee.
  • No facilities at the church — use the petrol station/café in Vik below.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Quick stop, 15-20 minutes total. Pair with a Vik village stop (fuel, toilets, lunch) and Reynisfjara.

  • Best light: Late afternoon for warm side-light on the church and Reynisdrangar.
  • Crowds: Light. Most south coast tours skip the church and stop only at Reynisfjara.
  • Combine with: Vik petrol/lunch stop, Reynisfjara, Dyrholaey.
  • Time budget: 15-20 min.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.6. Reviews are short and positive — "great view," "worth the 5 minute detour." Not a destination on its own, but a high-value-per-minute add-on to a Vik stop.

Honest take: Underrated as a quick photo stop. The church-plus-stacks composition is one of the more recognizable south coast images and takes 15 minutes.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Hilltop is windy; mind hats and tripods.
  • This is the designated Katla eruption evacuation point — if you ever hear a Katla alert during your visit, this is exactly where you should already be.
