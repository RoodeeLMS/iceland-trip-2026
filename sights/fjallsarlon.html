Fjallsarlon (Quieter Lagoon)

Day 4
📍 Coordinates64.0100, -16.3664
📅 Trip DayDay 4
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Fjallsárlón is the smaller, quieter sibling of Jokulsarlon — about 10 minutes' drive west on the Ring Road. It sits at the foot of Fjallsjökull, an outlet glacier of Vatnajökull, with the wall of ice dropping almost directly into the lagoon. Bergs are smaller and the lagoon is more compact, but you can stand significantly closer to the glacier face than at Jokulsarlon, and there are far fewer people. The on-site operator runs zodiac boat tours that — unlike Jokulsarlon's — start in early April.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round · Frost Restaurant and visitor building open. Zodiac boat tours operate from April 1 each year (per fjallsarlon.is) — book ahead for early-April slots.

April 2026 conditions: Lagoon is ice-rich and the glacier is active. Daylight ~14 hrs. Wind off the glacier face is significant — bring windproof layers. The short walk from the lot to the viewpoint can be muddy in April thaw; trail shoes are fine.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Turn off Route 1 onto the Fjallsárlón access road Signposted "Fjallsárlón." Short paved/gravel access road leads to a free dirt parking lot at Frost Restaurant. No Parka payment here — parking is currently free.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Frost Restaurant lot
2
Stop in Frost Restaurant — book the boat or grab coffee Frost is the buffet-style restaurant and tour office in one. If you decided to do the zodiac, this is where you check in. If you're just sightseeing, the soup-and-bread pit-stop here (~฿700-1,000) is a useful Day 4 lunch option. Toilets here.
⏱️ 10-15 min · 📍 Frost building
3
Walk the short trail to the lagoon overlook From the lot, follow the marked path ~5-10 minutes (~400 m, slight rise) over a low moraine ridge. The lagoon opens up suddenly with the glacier wall directly opposite — much closer than Jokulsarlon's ice face. This is the main viewpoint.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Main overlook, ~400m from lot
4
Walk along the shoreline path for closer berg views A gravel/dirt path continues along the lagoon edge. Walk 5-10 min in either direction for varied angles. You'll hear cracks and splashes from the calving glacier — much louder here because you're closer.
⏱️ 15-20 min · 📍 Shoreline path
5
Optional: Zodiac tour (book ahead) If you booked the zodiac, return to Frost on time. They issue dry suits, life jackets, and brief you. The tour runs ~1.5 hours total, ~45 min on the water. The boat takes you within ~50 m of the glacier wall — much closer than Jokulsarlon's amphibian. Per Iceland Adventure Tours: ~ISK 11,300 ≈ ฿2,825 per adult.
⏱️ 1.5 hrs if booked · 📍 Departs Frost dock
6
Drive on to Jokulsarlon (10 min east) Back on Route 1, Jokulsarlon and Diamond Beach are 10 minutes east. Doing Fjallsárlón first works well: smaller and quieter as a warmup, then the dramatic main event.
⏱️ 10 min drive

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Glacier-wall close-up: 24-70 mm from the main overlook — the glacier face is much closer here than at Jokulsarlon, so even a normal lens fills the frame.
  • Wide foreground berg: 16-24 mm low to the water on the shoreline path. Smaller bergs but better foreground geometry.
  • From the zodiac: Lock your camera strap. 24-70 mm is the do-everything lens; the boat gets close enough that wider-than-24mm distorts the ice oddly.
  • Best light: Late afternoon hits the glacier wall warm. Mornings are flat but quieter.

🚫 Drone: PERMIT REQUIRED — no-fly. Fjallsárlón is inside Vatnajökull National Park. Recreational drone use requires advance written authorization from park authorities. Don't fly. Cite: vatnajokulsthjodgardur.is. General rules: Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Short signposted access road off Route 1, ~10 minutes west of Jokulsarlon. Any car, no 4x4 needed.

  • Parking: Free at Frost Restaurant lot.
  • Walk to lagoon overlook: ~400 m, 5-10 min, slight rise over a low moraine. Trail shoes recommended in April mud.
  • Facilities: Frost Restaurant (toilets, food, tour booking) at the lot.
  • Cost: Free to visit. Zodiac boat tour: ~ISK 11,300 ≈ ฿2,825/adult. Booking strongly recommended ahead in early April.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Normal-priority detour. Most groups treat it as either an alternative or a warmup to Jokulsarlon. Allocate 45-60 min for sightseeing only, 2-2.5 hours if doing the zodiac.

  • Best light: Late afternoon for warm tone on the glacier wall.
  • Crowds: A fraction of Jokulsarlon's at any time of day. Even midday is bearable.
  • Combine with: Always paired with Jokulsarlon (10 min east), Diamond Beach (15 min east).
  • Day 4 sequencing: Skaftafell hike → Fjallsárlón (lunch + optional zodiac) → Jokulsarlon → Diamond Beach → Vestrahorn sunset → Höfn.
  • Skip if: You're already short on time. Jokulsarlon alone delivers 90% of the experience.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Tripadvisor and Google: ~4.7 / 5. Far fewer reviews than Jokulsarlon, but consistently positive. Visitors who do the zodiac tour rate it higher than Jokulsarlon's own boat tours because of how close the boat gets to the glacier wall.

Honest take: Underrated. If you only have time for one, Jokulsarlon is the bigger spectacle. But if you want a quieter experience and the chance to do an April boat tour (Jokulsarlon's don't run until May), Fjallsárlón is the answer. The shoreline walk feels much more intimate than the bus-park atmosphere at Jokulsarlon.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

  • Fjallsárlón Iceberg Boat Tours — official on-site operator. Zodiac classic boat tour ~1.5 hrs, ~ISK 11,300 ≈ ฿2,825/adult. Book ahead in April. Site: fjallsarlon.is/iceberg-boat-tours.
  • Frost Restaurant — buffet bistro, soup/salad/coffee. Useful Day 4 lunch stop.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Do NOT step onto icebergs or the lagoon ice. Same rules as Jokulsarlon.
  • Stay back from the lagoon edge — banks can collapse, water is ~2°C.
  • Wind chill: Glacier-wall wind is brutal even on a calm day.
  • Calving: Ice can fall off the glacier face without warning. Don't approach the wall on foot — only the zodiac goes near it.
  • Zodiac tours: Cancellation due to wind/weather is common in April. Have a buffer in your day's plan.
