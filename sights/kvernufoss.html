Kvernufoss

Day 3

📅 Trip DayDay 3

📖 Overview

Kvernufoss is a 30 m hidden waterfall in a small canyon directly behind the Skogar Folk Museum, only ~1 km east of the much busier Skogafoss. It's reached by a short, easy walk and offers something Skogafoss does not: a genuine walk-behind alcove. Far fewer people make the detour, so even at peak season you may have it to yourself for stretches.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open — No closures reported. The walk-behind path is on rocks/scree; in April there may still be snow patches and the rocks are slick. Approach the alcove cautiously and skip the walk-behind if footing looks bad.

April 2026 conditions: Trail is muddy in spots; waterproof boots help. Falls are at high spring flow.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the Skogar Folk Museum lotFrom Route 1, turn off at Skogar same as for Skogafoss but continue past it to the museum (~1 km east). Parking at the museum lot is free for visitors. Even if you're not going into the museum, the lot is the trailhead.
⏱️ 3 min · 📍 Skogar Folk Museum lot
2
Walk through the gate behind the museumBehind the museum buildings there's a stile/gate that crosses a fence into a sheep field. This is the official trail entrance — easy to miss. Cross the stile and pick up the obvious path along the river.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Stile behind museum
3
Follow the river upstream into the canyonThe trail follows the small Kverna river upstream into a narrow side canyon. ~1 km flat walk, ~15 minutes. The canyon walls close in and the falls reveal themselves at the head.
⏱️ 15 min · 📍 Canyon trail
4
Approach the falls and assess the walk-behindAt the head of the canyon the falls drop into a green amphitheater. Walk around the right (south) side of the pool toward the back wall. There's a ledge behind the falling water — in April assess carefully whether the rocks are dry enough to scramble across. If they're icy or wet, shoot from the side and skip the walk-behind.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Falls base
5
Walk-behind alcove (conditions permitting)If the ledge is safe, scramble the ~10 m across to the alcove behind the falling water. The view out through the curtain is the reason to come here. Shoot quickly — spray is heavy and footing is precarious.
⏱️ 5-10 min · 📍 Behind the falls
6
Walk back to the lotSame trail back. Total round trip: 50-70 minutes.
⏱️ 15 min walk · 📍 Return to museum lot

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Frame from inside the alcove out: 16-24 mm wide, looking out through the curtain of water with the green canyon behind. This is the signature shot — and the reason to do the walk-behind at all.
  • Side profile from the south bank: 35-50 mm at the pool edge, captures the entire falls + canyon wall.
  • Long exposure with ND filter at 1-2 sec works well here because the canyon is sheltered from wind.
  • Best light: Mid-afternoon when the sun reaches over the canyon rim. Mornings leave the alcove in deep shadow.
  • 🚫 Drone: Discouraged — narrow canyon, walking distance from the museum which has visitors, and disturbs the few people who made the effort to find solitude here.

🚗 Getting There

Trailhead at the Skogar Folk Museum lot, 1 km east of Skogafoss. Paved Route 1 + short paved access road. Any car.

  • Parking: free at the museum lot.
  • Walk to falls: ~1 km, 15 min on flat trail through a sheep field and along a small canyon.
  • No entry fee for the falls (museum admission separate).
  • Toilets: at the museum.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Always paired with Skogafoss and/or Skogar Folk Museum. A 60-minute detour total.

  • Best light: Mid-afternoon (13:00-15:00) when sun reaches into the canyon.
  • Crowds: Far thinner than Skogafoss. Often empty even at peak hours. The "you might have it to yourself" promise is real.
  • Combine with: Skogafoss (1 km west), Skogar Folk Museum (same lot), Lava Centre (~45 min west).
  • Time budget: 60-75 min for the round trip walk + falls time.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.7. Reviews repeatedly use the words "hidden gem" and "worth the walk." The canyon setting and the empty alcove (when accessible) are the headline. Common complaint is muddy trail in shoulder seasons.

Honest take: Underrated and especially valuable on this trip given that the Seljalandsfoss walk-behind is closed. Kvernufoss is your only realistic walk-behind opportunity on the south coast in April 2026 — assuming the alcove ledge is dry enough to cross.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities. Combined museum + falls visit takes 1.5-2 hours.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Slick wet rocks at the alcove ledge — assess carefully before attempting the walk-behind. Skip it if uncertain.
  • Trail can be muddy and slippery in spring; waterproof boots help.
  • Sheep are in the field — close gates behind you, don't disturb them.
  • Spring flow is heavy; spray reaches farther than in summer.
