Gljufrabui

Day 3
📍 Coordinates63.6194, -19.9872
📅 Trip DayDay 3
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Gljufrabui ("Canyon Dweller") is a 40 m waterfall hidden inside a narrow rock cleft 500 m north of Seljalandsfoss along the same cliff line. From outside you only see the top spilling over; to actually see the falls you wade up the shallow stream into the slot canyon. Most visitors miss it entirely because they don't know it's there — that's the appeal.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open — No active closures as of April 2026. The slot canyon entry is via wading the stream; in spring the water is cold and ankle-deep to mid-calf. Waterproof boots strongly recommended.

April 2026 conditions: Snowmelt makes the stream slightly higher than summer flow. The boulders inside the cleft are slick with algae and constant spray. The cave interior is permanently misted — assume your camera and hood will get wet.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Walk from Seljalandsfoss lotFrom the main Seljalandsfoss parking, head north along the obvious cliff-base path past two minor waterfalls. ~500 m on flat gravel, 5-7 minutes. No separate parking needed.
⏱️ 7 min · 📍 Path north of Seljalandsfoss
2
Spot the cleft entranceLook for a narrow vertical crack in the cliff with a stream running out of it. There's no signage shouting at you — easy to walk past. A worn boulder at the entrance is the giveaway.
⏱️ 1 min · 📍 Cleft entrance, 63.6194, -19.9872
3
Wade into the slot canyonStep into the stream and walk ~20 m into the cleft. The water is shallow (ankle-to-shin) but icy. Boots help; barefoot is miserable in April. Once inside the cleft opens into a hidden chamber where the full waterfall is revealed crashing onto a midstream boulder.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Inside the cleft
4
Climb the central boulder (carefully)A flat-topped boulder sits in the center of the chamber and is the classic photo spot. It's slick — three points of contact climbing up. From here the waterfall fills your entire field of view from above.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Central boulder
5
Optional: climb the chain route for the top viewOutside the cleft, on the south side, a short chain-assisted scramble climbs to a hole in the rock that gives a top-down view down into the canyon. Slick in spring — skip if uncertain.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 South side chain route
6
Walk back to Seljalandsfoss lotSame path back. Total round trip including time inside: 25-40 minutes.
⏱️ 7 min walk · 📍 Return to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • From the central boulder, ultra-wide: 14-20 mm pointed up to capture both the falling water and the slot of sky overhead. The cave is dim — bump ISO to 1600+ or use a tripod (good luck — it's wet and crowded).
  • Low angle from stream level: Crouch at the edge of the boulder, mid-tele 35-50 mm, frame the boulder as foreground.
  • Cleft entrance from outside: Wide angle showing the crack in the cliff with people for scale — emphasizes the "hidden" nature.
  • Camera protection is the entire game. Plastic bag, lens cloth, blower bulb. Lenses fog instantly going from cold to wet; let gear acclimate.

🚫 No drones inside the cleft (impossible anyway). Outside the cleft the same Seljalandsfoss-area discouragement applies — heavy crowds, narrow valley. See the Planning page drone section for general rules.

🚗 Getting There

No separate parking — share the Seljalandsfoss lot (ISK 800 ≈ ฿200). The 500 m walk along the cliff base is flat and obvious.

  • Walking shoes: Waterproof boots highly recommended for the stream wade. Sneakers will get soaked.
  • No fee for entering the cleft.
  • No facilities at Gljufrabui itself — use the toilets at Seljalandsfoss.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Always paired with Seljalandsfoss as a single visit. Total combined time: 60-90 minutes for both.

  • Best light: Midday in the chamber — direct overhead light from the slot above gives the strongest contrast on the falling water. Morning and evening leave the interior too dark.
  • Crowds: Far thinner than Seljalandsfoss because most visitors don't know it exists or don't want to wade. The cleft itself fits maybe 6-8 people comfortably; brief waits at peak hours.
  • Time budget: 20-30 min after the Seljalandsfoss visit.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.7. The "hidden gem" framing is universal in reviews — people who find it consistently rank it more impressive than Seljalandsfoss itself because of the dramatic enclosed setting. Common complaint: wet feet and slippery rocks for unprepared visitors.

Honest take: Underrated. If you'll already be at Seljalandsfoss with the walk-behind closed, Gljufrabui partially compensates by giving you the immersive close-up experience that the closed Seljalandsfoss path normally provides.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities. No tours specifically target this spot.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Slippery boulders inside the cleft — three points of contact climbing the central rock.
  • Cold water in April — wading without waterproof boots leads to numb feet fast.
  • Falling rock from the cleft walls is rare but possible; do not linger directly under the lip.
  • Camera gear gets soaked — protect or accept the risk.
  • The chain-assisted top route is slick and exposed in spring — skip if you're uncertain.
