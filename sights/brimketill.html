Brimketill Lava Pool

Day 9
📍 Coordinates63.8406, -22.6111
📅 Trip DayDay 9
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Brimketill ("surf cauldron") is a small natural pool eroded out of soft lava rock at the edge of a cliff on the south coast of Reykjanes, about 10 km west of Grindavík on Route 425. Atlantic swell pours into and out of the pool with each wave, creating spectacular spouts and white-water bursts. There's a wooden viewing platform at the cliff edge — that is the entire visit. Multiple tourists have been swept off the rocks here and killed. Per safetravel.is and Visit Reykjanes, do not climb down to the pool.

🚦 Current Status

🟠 Reykjanes volcanic system on Heightened Unrest (Level 2) — Per the Icelandic Met Office, no eruption underway in April 2026 but ~21 million m³ of magma beneath nearby Svartsengi. Route 425 is open. Brimketill itself is not within an evacuated zone, but the area can be closed at short notice if seismic activity escalates. Check safetravel.is and vedur.is the morning of your visit.

The viewing platform, parking lot, and signage are open and intact. SafeTravel maintains a permanent warning pin here for "dangerous waves and unpredictable surf." April brings the strongest spring swell of the year — wave action will be at its most spectacular and most dangerous. Wind chill on the exposed cliff edge is brutal in early April; bring a windproof shell.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the small lot on Route 425 Free gravel lot with a few bays directly off Route 425. Sign at the entrance. From the lot you can already hear the waves. Zip up before walking to the platform — wind off the Atlantic is constant.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Lot directly off Route 425
2
Walk the boardwalk to the cliff-edge platform A short, flat boardwalk (~80 m) leads from the lot to the viewing platform at the cliff edge. The platform is a railed wooden deck right above Brimketill itself. Stop at the rail; do not lean over.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Boardwalk, 80 m from lot
3
Watch a full wave cycle — count to 30 The pool fills and drains with the surge. Count one full cycle so you understand the rhythm. Big sets come in groups; the largest wave of a set may be 2-3x the average. If a "calm" pause makes you think you could nip closer, that's exactly when sneaker waves strike. Stay on the platform.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Viewing platform
4
Look west along the cliff line Walk to the western end of the platform. The cliff line continues as a series of jagged lava ridges with surf exploding through gaps. On a good swell day this is more dramatic than the pool itself. Telephoto lens helps.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 West end of platform
5
Back to the car — total visit ~15-20 min There is nothing else to do here. No café, no other trail. If you find yourself wanting to climb down, leave instead. Continue west on Route 425 toward Gunnuhver and Reykjanesviti lighthouse (10 min drive).
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Walk back to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Slow shutter wave drain: Tripod on the platform, 1/4 sec, capture water draining out of the pool as silver streaks across black lava.
  • Wave-explosion freeze: 1/1000 sec or faster with a 70-200 mm tele to freeze surf bursting through the lava notches west of the platform.
  • Backlit spray: Late afternoon when the sun is low to the west — spray and mist glow gold against dark cliffs.
  • Wide context: 16-24 mm from the platform, including pool, cliff, and open ocean horizon.

🚁 Drone — possible but extremely risky. No formal no-fly zone, but constant onshore wind off the Atlantic gusts well above DJI sub-250 g aircraft limits. Salt spray will corrode motors. If you fly, launch from the parking lot, stay inland of the cliff line, and keep flights brief. If wind exceeds your aircraft's rating, do not launch. Reykjanes peninsula TFRs may apply during volcanic activity — check NOTAMs. General drone rules on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 425 (Nesvegur), 10 km west of Grindavík. About 50 minutes from Reykjavík, 30 minutes from KEF airport. Any 2WD rental.

  • Parking: Free gravel lot directly at the trailhead.
  • Walk: ~80 m on flat boardwalk to the platform.
  • Fees: None.
  • Facilities: None — no toilets, no shelter. Use Grindavík (10 min east) before driving out.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Brief stop on the western Reykjanes loop. Pair directly with Gunnuhver (10 min west) — they're essentially the same visit. Order: Brimketill → Gunnuhver → Reykjanesviti lighthouse → back east toward Blue Lagoon area.

  • Best light: Late afternoon for warm side-light on the cliffs. High swell days (winter and early spring) are most dramatic — April is peak.
  • Crowds: Light. Quick stop for tour buses; mostly self-driving travellers.
  • Allocate: 15-20 minutes. Longer if photographing seriously and surf is up.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps rating ~4.4/5. Common praise: dramatic surf, easy access, photogenic. Common complaint: "There's just a viewing platform, that's it" — true, and that's the entire point. Honest take: worth the 20-minute stop if you're already on the western Reykjanes loop, but not worth a special drive from Reykjavík on its own.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

Per safetravel.is and Visit Reykjanes:

  • Stay on the viewing platform. Do not climb down to the pool. Tourists have been killed here.
  • Sneaker waves and surge can drag a person off the rocks in seconds — there is no second chance.
  • Strong, gusty winds can knock you off balance even on the platform. Hold the railing.
  • Lava rocks below the platform are razor-sharp and slippery from spray.
  • No safety supervision. No mobile coverage in some pockets.
  • Do not let children climb on or beyond the railing.
  • Reykjanes volcanic monitoring active — check vedur.is and safetravel.is the morning of your visit.
