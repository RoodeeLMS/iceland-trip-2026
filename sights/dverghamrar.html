📖 Overview

Dverghamrar ("Dwarf Cliffs") is a small cluster of basalt columns directly off Route 1, ~10 km east of Kirkjubaejarklaustur. The columns are arranged in two distinct layers — the lower section is regular hexagonal basalt and the upper section is "cube-jointed" basalt that fractured into more chaotic shapes. Folklore holds that dwarves and elves live among the rocks. It's a 5-10 minute roadside curiosity — not a destination, but a free, easy add-on between Eldhraun and Fjadrargljufur.

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Vertical wide of the column face: 16-24 mm tilted up to capture both basalt layers in one frame.
  • Person for scale: The columns are smaller than they look in photos — without a person in frame, viewers can't gauge the size.
  • Best light: Side-light early morning or late afternoon for column texture; midday is flat.
  • 🚫 Drone: Roadside stop, traffic close by — drone use not appropriate. Skip.

🚗 Getting There

Direct pull-off on Route 1, ~10 km east of Kirkjubaejarklaustur. Free parking. Any car.

  • Parking: free roadside pull-off.
  • No entry fee.
  • No facilities.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

5-minute roadside curiosity. Pair with Eldhraun (very nearby) and Fjadrargljufur (~10 min east).

  • Crowds: Almost always empty — most tour buses drive past without stopping.
  • Combine with: Eldhraun, Fjadrargljufur, Kirkjubaejarklaustur.
  • Time budget: 5-10 min.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.5. Reviews are short — "neat quick stop," "smaller than expected," "free and worth 5 minutes." Nobody's bucket-list sight, but nobody complains either.

Honest take: Worth the 5-minute stop because it's literally on Route 1 between Eldhraun and Fjadrargljufur — not worth a detour, but zero reason to skip when you're driving past.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Watch for traffic at the highway pull-off.
  • Do not climb the columns — basalt edges are sharp and the rocks are unstable in spots.
  • Loose rock at the base; mind your footing.
