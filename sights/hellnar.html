Hellnar & Fjöruhúsið Café

Day 7★ Star
📍 Coordinates64.7506, -23.6483
📅 Trip DayDay 7
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Hellnar is a tiny former fishing hamlet 2.5 km west of Arnarstapi at the foot of Snæfellsjökull. Two reasons to come: (1) the coastal walking trail connecting Arnarstapi to Hellnar through lava cliffs, sea arches, blowholes, and sea bird colonies — one of the best short coastal walks in Iceland; and (2) Fjöruhúsið, a tiny harbor café famous for its fish soup, which reopens for the season at Easter.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Trail open year-round. Fjöruhúsið café opens for the 2026 season at Easter (April 5), so it will be open during our trip. Hours typically 11:00-17:00; verify on the day.

April 2026: Trail is fine but sections can be icy in shaded lava hollows — wear shoes with grip. Wind on the cliff edge is the real concern; do not approach the edge in gusts above 20 m/s.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at Arnarstapi (recommended) or HellnarThe classic plan is to park at Arnarstapi — bigger lot, easier access, more to see — and walk one-way to Hellnar. If you have two cars do a shuttle; if not, plan to walk back. Solo car: park at Arnarstapi and either walk a there-and-back (2-2.5 hrs) or just drive to Hellnar separately for the café.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Arnarstapi parking lot (paid, ~฿250)
2
Walk the coastal trail to HellnarThe marked trail leaves the back of the Arnarstapi lot, heading west. Follow it through lava fields along the cliff edge. Total: 2.5 km, ~30-45 min one way at a normal pace, longer if you stop for photos (you will). The path is mostly flat with a few gentle ups and downs. Stay on the marked trail — the lava is fragile and the cliff edges crumble.
⏱️ 30-45 min · 📍 Marked trail Arnarstapi → Hellnar
3
Stop at the Gatklettur arch (~1 km in)About 1/3 of the way along is Gatklettur, a perfect circular sea arch eroded into the basalt cliff. Best photo spot of the entire walk — peer through the arch at the breaking surf below. Stay back from the unfenced edge.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Gatklettur arch, ~1 km W of Arnarstapi
4
Watch for sea birdsIn April you may see early-arriving fulmars and kittiwakes nesting on the cliff faces. Puffins arrive late April / early May — possibly visible by trip end. Bring binoculars if you have them.
⏱️ Continuous · 📍 Cliff edge
5
Arrive at Hellnar harborThe trail descends to a tiny stone harbor with the Fjöruhúsið café perched right above the rocks. The café is in an old converted fisherman's shack — 6-8 tables, sea view through every window.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Hellnar harbor
6
Lunch at Fjöruhúsið — fish soupThe famous fish soup (~ISK 2,800 / ฿700) is the order. Tomato-based, generously stocked with white fish, served with bread. Cakes and coffee also good. Bring cash or card — they take both. Arrive before 13:00 to beat the lunch crunch.
⏱️ 30-45 min · 📍 Fjöruhúsið café, Hellnar
7
Walk back (or arrange a pickup)If you walked there, the return is the same trail — and it looks completely different in the opposite direction lighting. Allow another 30-45 min. If energy is low, the road between Hellnar and Arnarstapi is a 5-min drive — pre-arrange a pickup with a co-traveler.
⏱️ 30-45 min · 📍 Trail back to Arnarstapi
8
Bonus: walk down to the Hellnar churchBefore leaving, the small black-roofed Hellnar church sits on a rise just above the harbor. 2-min detour, photogenic against Snæfellsjökull behind it on a clear day.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Hellnar church, 200 m from café

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Gatklettur arch: Wide 16-24mm framing the arch with the surf through it. Slow shutter (1/8 s on tripod) blurs the wave action below.
  • Fjöruhúsið café: The red-roofed shack from the harbor stones below makes the iconic Hellnar shot. Late afternoon light hits it best.
  • Snæfellsjökull behind Hellnar: On a clear day, walk 50 m east of the café for an unobstructed glacier-volcano backdrop above the village.
  • Bird-on-cliff telephoto: 100-400mm if you have it — fulmars in April are habituated and let you get close.

🚫 Drone: Per multiple Iceland drone-rule guides (2025-2026), the entire Snæfellsnes coastline near nesting cliffs has informal seasonal restrictions during seabird breeding (May-Aug). In early April you are technically before the restriction window, but birds are arriving — fly only if no birds are visible on the cliffs and stay well away from the edge. The Snæfellsjökull National Park boundary is just west of Hellnar; do not fly inside park land. See Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

  • Paved Route 574 from Arnarstapi to Hellnar (5 min drive) or via the coastal trail (30-45 min walk).
  • Arnarstapi parking: paid, ~ISK 1,000 / ฿250 via Parka app.
  • Hellnar parking: small free lot at the harbor turnaround.
  • Trail: marked, 2.5 km, mostly flat lava terrain, sturdy shoes essential.
  • No fees for the trail or the village.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Time needed: 2.5-3 hours total if doing the full walk + café lunch + walk back.
  • Best time: Late morning — start the walk ~10:30, arrive Hellnar for early lunch ~12:00 before the café fills.
  • Combine with: Arnarstapi (the trail starts there), Búðakirkja (20 min east), Djúpalónssandur (20 min west).
  • If short on time: Drive directly to Hellnar, eat at the café, walk only 500 m of the trail east toward Gatklettur and back. 1 hour total.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google ~4.7 for the café, ~4.8 for the coastal trail. Café reviewers consistently call the fish soup "best in Iceland" or "best on Snæfellsnes" — high praise that mostly holds up. Honest take: the soup is genuinely excellent and the setting (tiny shack on the rocks) is unbeatable. The trail review consensus is that it is the single best short coastal walk in west Iceland — better than most paid tour stops. The only complaint: limited seating at the café; arrive early or expect a wait.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. Just walk in to Fjöruhúsið; no reservations.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Cliff edges along the trail are unfenced and crumbling — stay on the marked path and keep small kids hand-held.
  • Wind: gusts above 20 m/s make the cliff edge dangerous. Check Vedur.is wind forecast the morning of.
  • Lava is sharp — a fall on it cuts deep. Sturdy shoes mandatory.
  • No SafeTravel.is pin specific to Hellnar at time of research, but the Snæfellsnes coastal cliffs in general have logged fall fatalities.
