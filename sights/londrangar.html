Lóndrangar Basalt Cliffs

Day 7
📍 Coordinates64.7383, -23.7817
📅 Trip DayDay 7
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions▶️ Watch on YouTube

📖 Overview

Lóndrangar are two dramatic basalt sea stacks — 75 m and 61 m tall — rising out of the cliff line on the south coast of Snæfellsnes inside the national park. They are the eroded plugs of an old volcanic crater. A wooden viewing platform sits 200 m from the parking lot, and a short clifftop walk leads further west for a side-on angle. It is one of the easiest "wow" stops on the peninsula and pairs naturally with Djúpalónssandur and Saxhóll on the same Day 7 loop.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free, in Snæfellsjökull National Park. Viewing platform with a railing — safe for all ages.

April 2026: Cliffs face south so wind exposure is constant. Path can be muddy/icy in shoulder season. Drone permit required in the national park.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the Malarrif / Lóndrangar lotSignposted off Route 574, free gravel lot. Two access points: the main Malarrif visitor centre (with toilets and a small exhibit on the park) and a smaller dedicated Lóndrangar lot 1 km further east. Either works.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 64.7383, -23.7817
2
Walk to the main viewing platformFrom the Lóndrangar lot, a flat 200 m gravel path leads to a wooden railed platform on the cliff edge directly opposite the two stacks. This is the headline view: both pillars rising vertically out of the dark cliff face.
⏱️ 4 min · 📍 200 m S of lot
3
Walk west along the clifftop for 5 minutesFrom the platform, an unmarked but obvious path runs ~300 m west along the cliff edge. The angle changes and the smaller stack lines up beside the larger — a more dramatic "twin stacks" composition. Stay back from the unfenced edge.
⏱️ 10 min round trip · 📍 ~300 m W
4
Optional: walk east toward Malarrif lighthouseFrom the same platform, a 1.5 km coastal path leads east to the orange Malarrif lighthouse and visitor centre. Adds ~45 min round trip. Skip in bad weather; do it if you have time and the light is good.
⏱️ 30-45 min · 📍 Optional, 1.5 km E
5
Read the bird & saga signs at the platformInformation boards at the platform explain the cliff seabird colonies (kittiwakes, fulmars, guillemots, puffins seasonally) and the local saga that the larger pillar is a "church" of the hidden people. 2 minutes of reading adds context.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Platform

📷 Photography & Drone

  • Headline shot: 24-50 mm from the main viewing platform. Both stacks vertical, cliff line as foreground.
  • Compression shot: 70-200 mm tele from the western clifftop, lining up the smaller stack against the larger.
  • Wide context: 16-24 mm from further back showing the full cliff sweep and offshore swell.
  • Best light: Late afternoon (cliffs face south-west). Sunset directly behind the stacks for silhouette.

🚁 Drone — PERMIT REQUIRED: Inside Snæfellsjökull National Park. Drone use requires a permit from the park warden (Umhverfisstofnun / snaefellsjokull.is). Cliffs are also a major seabird nesting site — additional courtesy concerns from May onward. Treat as no-fly. See Planning drone section.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 574, free gravel lot. Any rental car. ~5 min east of Djúpalónssandur, ~15 min south of Saxhóll.

  • Parking: Free, ~20 spaces at Lóndrangar lot, more at Malarrif visitor centre 1 km east.
  • Walk: 200 m flat gravel to the platform.
  • Fees: None.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 20-30 min for the platform; 60-75 min if you walk to Malarrif lighthouse and back.
  • Best light: Late afternoon.
  • Combine with: Djúpalónssandur (5 min west) and Saxhóll (15 min north). All three are the western tip Day 7 loop and easily done in a half day.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ~4.6 (4,000+ reviews). Praised as effortless and dramatic — minimal walking, big payoff. Honest complaints are mostly about wind and occasional fog hiding the stacks. Honest take: highest reward-per-effort stop on Snæfellsnes. Always include if passing.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. Malarrif visitor centre has a small free exhibit on the national park geology and wildlife.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Unfenced cliff edges past the platform — strong wind risk, stay well back.
  • Path can be slippery in wet/icy conditions.
  • Falling rock from cliff face — do not stand directly under overhangs (none on the platform itself).
