Brauð & Co Bakery

Day 1
📍 Coordinates64.1453, -21.9269
📅 Trip DayDay 1
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Brauð & Co is Reykjavík's most-photographed bakery — a small chain whose Frakkastígur 16 flagship sits at the foot of Hallgrímskirkja under a riot of street-art murals by Icelandic artist Hugleikur Dagsson. The draw is the cardamom snail (kanilsnúður with cardamom) and cinnamon snails, baked on-site in an open kitchen you can see from the sidewalk.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round. Frakkastígur location reported open daily 06:30–18:00 (verify on Google before walking over). No closures reported.

Cash-free; cards and Apple/Google Pay accepted. Often a short queue during 08:00-10:00 weekend tourist rush — line moves fast since most people grab a single pastry.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Walk up Frakkastígur from Laugavegur From the main shopping street, turn onto Frakkastígur and walk uphill toward Hallgrímskirkja — 3 minutes. The bakery is on the right, impossible to miss thanks to the graffiti mural covering the entire facade.
⏱️ 3 min · 📍 Frakkastígur 16
2
Order at the counter — go for the cardamom snail Point and pay. The cardamom snail (~ISK 700 / ฿175) is the signature; the cinnamon version is also excellent. Sourdough loaves, pain au chocolat, and rye bread also available. No table seating — it's grab-and-go.
⏱️ 5-10 min · 📍 Counter inside
3
Photograph the facade from across the street Cross Frakkastígur for the full mural shot. Best in morning light when the sun hits the front of the building. The tall blue/yellow cartoon figures are by Hugleikur Dagsson.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Sidewalk opposite
4
Walk 5 minutes uphill to Hallgrímskirkja Eat your snail on the way. Frakkastígur dead-ends at the church steps — the perfect natural pairing.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Hallgrímskirkja, 400m uphill

📷 Photography & Drone

Best shot: Cross to the opposite sidewalk and shoot the full mural facade with a 24-35mm lens. Morning sun (08:00-10:00) lights the front; later in the day the building is in shadow. For a person-in-front composition, position the subject just left of the entrance so the mural fills the frame.

🚫 Drone: No drones — this is dense central Reykjavík and within the city's blanket urban no-fly zone. See Planning page for details.

🚗 Getting There

  • Walking from anywhere in central Reykjavík: 5-15 min.
  • From Hallgrímskirkja: 5 min downhill on Frakkastígur.
  • No dedicated parking — use the paid street parking on nearby streets (Zone P2, ~฿70/hr) if driving.
  • No fees, no tickets — just walk in.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Best time: Right when they open at 06:30, or 09:00-10:00 once jet-lagged Day 1 starts. Pastries are warmest in the morning.
  • Crowds: Midday weekends are busiest. Early morning is calm.
  • Combine with: Hallgrímskirkja (5 min walk uphill), Laugavegur shopping street, Sun Voyager sculpture (10 min walk down to the harbour).
  • Time needed: 10-20 min total.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ~4.6 stars across thousands of reviews. Reykjavík Grapevine and most local food guides list the cardamom snail as "best in the city." Honest take: it lives up to the hype — the pastry is genuinely excellent and the price (~฿175) is fair by Reykjavík standards. The only complaint worth noting: no seating, so plan to eat on the go.

Self-guided — just walk in.
