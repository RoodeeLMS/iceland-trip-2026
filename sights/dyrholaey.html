Dyrholaey Viewpoint & Arch

📖 Overview

Dyrholaey ("door hill island") is a 120 m volcanic promontory at the southernmost tip of mainland Iceland, named for the massive natural sea arch piercing its base. The headland delivers some of the south coast's best panoramas: black-sand beaches stretching east to Reynisfjara, the Reynisdrangar sea stacks, the Mýrdalsjökull glacier inland, and on clear days the Westman Islands offshore. There are two parking areas — a low lot at sea level and a high lot atop the promontory — and they offer completely different experiences.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 OPEN in April 2026 — Dyrholaey is closed annually May 1 to June 25 for puffin and seabird nesting (per Environment Agency of Iceland). We're visiting in early April, so it's open. After the closure ends in late June, the upper lot is the only one accessible during nesting season.

April 2026 conditions: Wind on the upper headland is severe — expect 50-80 km/h gusts on most spring days. Holds onto your hat, secure tripods, and consider whether the upper lot is safe to walk if wind is in red-flag territory. The unpaved upper road can be muddy; 2WD is fine in dry but tricky after heavy rain.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Decide: lower lot or upper lot first?Lower lot (Kirkjufjara/Lágey): sea-level beach with views of the arch from underneath. Upper lot (Háey): top of the promontory with the lighthouse and panoramic views over the entire coast. If you only have time for one, the upper lot is the better choice for the panorama. With time for both, do upper first then drive down to lower.
⏱️ Decide before turning · 📍 Route 218 junction
2
Drive Route 218 — note: upper road is steeperTurn off Route 1 onto paved Route 218. After ~5 km the road splits: the upper lot road is signed and is a steeper, partly gravel climb. The lower lot continues straight. Both are doable in any 2WD car in dry conditions.
⏱️ 10 min drive · 📍 Route 218
3
Upper lot — walk to the lighthousePark at the upper lot (free). The 1910 Dyrholaey lighthouse sits at the highest point, ~200 m walk from the lot on a flat path. Wind can be brutal here — brace yourself.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Upper lot lighthouse
4
Walk to the cliff edge for the panoramaFrom the lighthouse, walk to the marked cliff-edge viewpoint. South: the Atlantic and (clear day) the Westman Islands. East: black sand beach stretching to Reynisfjara and the basalt sea stacks of Reynisdrangar in the distance. West: the long Solheimasandur black sand plain. Stay behind the rope/marked edge.
⏱️ 15-20 min · 📍 Cliff edge
5
Look down: spot the arch from aboveFrom the cliff edge you can see the natural arch piercing the base of the promontory directly below — it's big enough that boats and small planes have famously passed through. The view straight down into the surf is the headline.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Arch overlook
6
Drive down to the lower lot (optional)Back to Route 218 and continue to the lower lot. From here a short walk takes you to a sea-level beach where you see the arch from underneath — a different and equally photogenic view. The beach itself is dangerous (sneaker waves) and is sometimes closed; check signage. Stay well back from the water.
⏱️ 15 min · 📍 Lower lot beach
7
Photo: Reynisdrangar from the east edgeWhether at upper or lower lot, walk to the easternmost accessible point and frame the Reynisdrangar sea stacks 5 km east. This is one of the iconic south coast compositions.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 East edge

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Upper lot panorama, golden hour: 24-35 mm from the cliff edge facing east, late afternoon when the basalt stacks are side-lit. Best single composition on the south coast.
  • Tele compression toward Reynisdrangar: 70-200 mm at ~135 mm compresses the sea stacks against the surf. Stack the lighthouse in the foreground for a strong layered shot.
  • Arch from the lower beach: 16-24 mm wide at sea level, low tide is best to get the arch silhouetted against the sky. Black sand foreground.
  • Lighthouse: Backlit at sunset with the sun behind it from the west.
  • Wind is the biggest practical issue. Bring a heavy bag to weight the tripod, or shoot handheld at high shutter speeds.
  • 🚫 Drone: NO DRONES April through August due to bird nesting protections. Dyrholaey is a designated bird reserve and drone use is prohibited during the nesting season — and discouraged year-round. Do not fly here. See Planning page drone section for general rules.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 218 directly off Route 1, ~10 km west of Vik. The lower lot road is paved; the upper lot road is a steeper paved + short gravel climb. Both are accessible in any 2WD car in dry weather. After heavy rain or in snow, the upper road can be tricky — assess at the junction.

  • Parking: free at both lots.
  • No entry fee.
  • No facilities at either lot — no toilets, no café. Use the facilities in Vik (10 min east) before or after.
  • Walk from upper lot to lighthouse: ~200 m, flat.
  • Walk from lower lot to beach: ~150 m, flat.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Visiting Day 3 westbound and possibly again Day 5 on the return. The two-visit hedge is useful: if Day 3 is too windy or low-cloud, Day 5's forecast may be clear.

  • Best light: Late afternoon to sunset for the eastward Reynisdrangar shot. Sunrise also works but the sun rises behind the sea stacks, throwing them into silhouette.
  • Crowds: Lighter than Reynisfjara because of the extra detour. Buses do come but in smaller numbers.
  • Combine with: Reynisfjara (5 km east), Vik village (10 km east), Vik church viewpoint.
  • Time budget: 45 min for upper lot only; 75-90 min for both lots.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.7. Frequently called the best viewpoint on the south coast — the panoramic position lets you see most other south coast highlights from one spot. Common positives: lighthouse, panorama, fewer crowds than Reynisfjara. Common complaints: severe wind, no facilities, occasional closure of the lower road in spring.

Honest take: Underrated relative to Reynisfjara. The upper lot view is arguably the single best landscape shot on the south coast. Don't skip it just because Reynisfjara is the famous one.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities. Some Reykjavik day tours include a brief stop here, but with our rental car there's no reason to book one.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Severe wind on the upper headland — risk of being blown off balance near cliff edges. Stay behind ropes/markers.
  • Cliff drops are unfenced in places — keep children and groups well back.
  • The lower beach has the same sneaker-wave hazard as Reynisfjara — minimum 25 m back from the water.
  • Closed annually May 1 to June 25 for nesting birds — we're in April so this does not affect us, but plan around it for any future trips.
  • Upper road can be muddy or icy; check at the junction before committing.
