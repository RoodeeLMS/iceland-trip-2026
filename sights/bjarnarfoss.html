Bjarnarfoss

📖 Overview

Bjarnarfoss is a tall, thin two-tier waterfall on the southern edge of Snæfellsnes, visible for kilometers from Route 54 and directly above the Búðakirkja area. A 10-minute walk from the small parking lot leads to a viewing platform. Zero extra drive time if you are already stopping at Búðakirkja — they are essentially the same location.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free. Small parking lot off Route 54, marked path with a viewing platform built in 2018. No facilities.

April 2026: Path can be icy and muddy in places — micro-spikes optional but useful. Strong flow from snowmelt.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the marked Bjarnarfoss lot off Route 54Signed turnoff on the north side of Route 54, between Búðakirkja and Vegamót. ~10 cars, free.
⏱️ 1 min · 📍 Route 54 turnoff
2
Walk the gravel path to the viewing platformA maintained gravel path climbs gently for ~500 m / 10 min to a wooden viewing platform. Steep at the very end. The platform gives a head-on view of both tiers.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Viewing platform
3
Optional scramble higher for the upper-tier viewPast the platform a fainter trail continues another 200 m up loose scree to a closer view of the upper drop. Not maintained — only attempt if dry and you are sure-footed. Skip if wet or windy.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Upper scramble
4
Walk back & continue to BúðakirkjaBúðakirkja, the iconic black church, is a 5-min drive away. They pair as a single 45-min combined stop.
⏱️ +30 min · 📍 Búðakirkja, 5 min by car

📷 Photography & Drone

Best angle: From the viewing platform with a 24-50mm wide. For the iconic "waterfall above the black church" composition, shoot from the Búðakirkja side with a 70-200mm tele to compress the falls behind the church spire. That is the postcard shot.

Best light: Mid-afternoon — the cliff faces roughly south.

🚫 Drone: Open rural area, no posted restrictions. Generally OK in calm wind. Mind the wind funneling off the cliff. See Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

  • Paved Route 54, signed turnoff.
  • Free gravel parking, ~10 spaces.
  • 500 m / 10 min walk on a maintained path.
  • Any car, no 4x4.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Time needed: 30 min including the walk; 45 min combined with Búðakirkja.
  • Combine with: Búðakirkja (5 min drive — directly pair them), Gerðuberg (30 min east), Arnarstapi/Hellnar (25 min west).

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google ~4.6. Reviewers note "underrated, easy walk, no crowds." Real complaint: the upper scramble is sketchier than reviews sometimes suggest — stick to the official platform unless you are sure of conditions.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

Path is icy in spring; wear grippy shoes. Upper scramble is loose scree — avoid in wet or windy conditions.
