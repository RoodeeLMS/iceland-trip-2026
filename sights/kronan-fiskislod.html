Kronan Fiskislod Grocery

Day 1
📍 Coordinates64.1548, -21.9516
📅 Trip DayDay 1
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Not a sight — a logistics stop. Kronan is one of Iceland's two main budget grocery chains (the other being Bonus). The Fiskislod / Grandi branch sits in the Old Harbour district about 10 minutes from downtown by car, and it's the most convenient large supermarket for stocking up on road-trip food before heading out on Day 2 onward. Stocking your car here will save you ฿2,000-4,000 per day versus eating at every petrol station and tourist restaurant on the south coast.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open daily. Per kronan.is and já.is: standard hours are roughly 09:00 (or 10:00) – 21:00 daily, including Sundays. Fiskislod address: Fiskislod 15-21, 101 Reykjavik. Confirm exact hours on the day via Google Maps.

Cards accepted (Visa/MC), no minimum. The Krónan app gives small discounts but is not necessary.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park out front and grab a basket Free parking lot directly in front of the store — easy in/out, plenty of space, much less stressful than central Reykjavik. Walk in, grab a basket (no coin needed for baskets; trolleys may need a 100 ISK coin or app).
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Free lot, Fiskislod 15-21
2
Hit the essentials list What to actually buy for the road trip (priced roughly in ฿):
  • Skyr — single-serve pots, ฿60-90 each. Buy 8-10 for breakfasts.
  • Bread + cheese + cold cuts — sandwich kit for car lunches. ~฿800 covers 4-5 meals.
  • Bottled water — large 1.5 L bottles ฿50-80. Buy a 6-pack and refill from taps thereafter (Iceland's tap water is the best in the world).
  • Snacks — Icelandic chocolate (Sirius, Noi Sirius), licorice, bananas, apples, trail mix.
  • Instant noodles / cup soups if your hotels have kettles.
  • Disposable cutlery + paper towels for car eating.
  • Coffee or tea bags if you want morning caffeine in your room.
Skip: alcohol (sold separately at Vinbudin, and your group doesn't drink), prepared hot meals (limited selection here).
⏱️ 20-30 min · 📍 In-store
3
Pay and load directly into the car Self-checkout and staffed lanes both available. Bring or buy reusable bags (~฿25 each — plastic bags are not free in Iceland). Load directly into the rental and you're set for the next 2-3 days of road tripping.
⏱️ 5-10 min

📷 Photography & Drone

Not a photo stop. It's a grocery store. Drones obviously not relevant — and central Reykjavik / Old Harbour is BIRK controlled airspace anyway.

🚗 Getting There

  • From downtown Reykjavik: 5-10 minutes by car. Head north-west on Geirsgata / Mýrargata into the Grandi area; the store is on Fiskislod.
  • Parking: Free, large lot in front. The whole point of choosing this branch over a downtown one.
  • Walking: Possible from Old Harbour (~15 min) but not really practical with a full grocery load.
  • Fees: None to enter. Bag fees ~฿25.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Best time: Late afternoon Day 1, after picking up the rental car and before dinner. Or Day 2 morning before leaving Reykjavik for the Golden Circle.
  • Combine with: Grandi old harbour area has good restaurants (Matur og Drykkur, Coocoo's Nest) and the Whales of Iceland museum if you want a half-day in the area.
  • Allocate: 30-45 minutes total including parking and loading.
  • Bonus tip: Bonus (the orange pig logo store) is marginally cheaper than Krónan, but Krónan has a better selection and more central locations. Either is fine. Avoid 10-11 (the convenience chain) for groceries — it's roughly 30-50% more expensive.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps: ~4.4 / 5. Locals like Krónan as a slightly higher-quality alternative to Bonus, with better produce and a wider selection of organic/vegan options. Per Guide to Iceland, prices are similar to Bonus but selection is broader.

Honest take: It's a supermarket. Doing a grocery run on Day 1 and stocking the car is one of the highest-leverage cost-saving moves on any Iceland trip — easily ฿10,000-20,000 saved over a 9-day trip versus eating out for every meal.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-serve grocery shopping. No tickets, tours, or activities.

⚠️ Safety Notes

None. Reykjavik supermarket — same risk profile as a Tesco. Watch the parking lot for slick patches in early April.
