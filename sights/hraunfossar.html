Hraunfossar (Lava Falls)

Day 6★ Priority
📍 Coordinates64.7022, -20.9789
📅 Trip DayDay 6 (West / Silver Circle)
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Hraunfossar — literally "Lava Falls" — is a ~900 m wide curtain of hundreds of small streams emerging directly from underneath the Hallmundarhraun lava field and pouring into the turquoise Hvita river. The water has filtered through porous lava from springs further up the field, so the falls have no visible source above the cliff edge. It is unique in Iceland: not one big drop but a continuous shimmering wall of clear rivulets against black basalt and (in spring) yellow moss.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free, no closures — Per icelandtours.is and icelandia.com: paved access, free parking, viewing platforms accessible all year.

April 2026 conditions: Snow is typically receding by early April but the wooden viewing platform and connecting paths can be icy in the morning — micro-spikes recommended. The river runs high from spring melt, so the falls are at peak volume. The surrounding lava field still has patches of snow which actually enhance the contrast against the black basalt and emerging streams.

Hraunfossar Cafe at the parking lot is generally open for the spring shoulder season but hours can be limited — don't count on it for a meal, plan to bring snacks.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the Hraunfossar lot off Route 518 Free paved parking, restrooms (1,000 ISK / ~฿250 cash only per Iceland with a View), and the small Hraunfossar Cafe. The lot is the same one used for Barnafoss — you do both sights in one stop. Lock the car; this lot is occasionally hit by minor break-ins (per Tripadvisor reviews).
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Hraunfossar parking, off Rd 518
2
Walk to the main viewing platform From the lot, follow the signposted gravel path ~80 m to the wooden deck directly above the falls. This is the iconic wide composition: the entire ~900 m curtain of streams emerging from the lava cliff face, with the Hvita's milky turquoise water below. Stop here for the orientation shot before walking the longer path.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Main viewing deck, ~80 m from lot
3
Walk the upper path west along the cliff edge Follow the railed path west (away from Barnafoss) for ~200 m. Multiple side viewpoints let you isolate single rivulets or small clusters — the light-and-water detail at close range is what separates this stop from a generic waterfall. Look for the moss colour: even in April there are patches of vivid yellow-green on the lava surface above the streams.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Upper cliff path, ~200 m west
4
Cross the footbridge over the Hvita and look back Return to the main deck and continue east. A short footbridge crosses the gorge between Hraunfossar and Barnafoss. From mid-bridge, look back west — this is the classic side-on view showing the full vertical scale of the lava cliff and the streams stacked along it. Best single composition of the visit.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Footbridge between the two falls
5
Continue 5 min to Barnafoss The marked path continues east along the river to Barnafoss (see the separate Barnafoss page on this site). 5 minute walk — combine the two as one ~45 minute total visit.
⏱️ 5 min walk · 📍 Barnafoss, ~300 m east

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Wide curtain shot from the footbridge: 16-24 mm wide-angle, side-on, shows the full vertical lava cliff and the streams stacked along it. Single best composition of the stop.
  • Detail shots from the upper path: 70-200 mm tele to isolate clusters of two or three rivulets against black basalt — strong abstract compositions. Try 1/15-1/4 sec shutter on a tripod for silky water with the moss still sharp.
  • Best light: Morning (low east light rakes the cliff face). Around midday the sun is overhead and the cliff goes flat. Overcast is actually fine here — the turquoise water reads better without harsh sun.
  • Reflection: Low spots along the river give clean reflections of the lava cliff in the calmer sections downstream of the main falls.

🚁 Drone: No site-specific ban posted — Hraunfossar is not in a national park or seabird zone. Wind funnels through the river gorge so launch from the parking lot side, not the cliff edge. Stay clear of the viewing decks if anyone is on them. This is one of the better drone spots on the trip if the wind cooperates — top-down of the streams emerging from the lava is a unique aerial. For general Iceland drone law see the Drone section on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

~1.5 hours from Reykjavik via Ring Road then Route 50 then Route 518. All paved, any 2WD rental fine. ~30 min off the Ring Road.

  • Parking: Free (per Iceland with a View).
  • Restrooms: 1,000 ISK ≈ ฿250, cash only.
  • Walk to falls: ~80 m flat gravel from lot to main deck.
  • Entry: Free.
  • Same lot serves Barnafoss — do both in one stop.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Priority ★ sight on Day 6's West Iceland / Silver Circle loop. Pair tightly with Barnafoss (5 min walk) and Deildartunguhver (~30 min drive south). Total combined Hraunfossar + Barnafoss visit: ~45 min.

  • Best light: Morning. Plan to arrive 9-11 to catch low east light on the cliff face.
  • Crowds: Much lighter than the Golden Circle — even in summer this is "hidden gem" territory. April shoulder = essentially no one.
  • Combine with: Barnafoss (immediate), Deildartunguhver hot spring (~30 km south), Husafell area, optional Vidgelmir lava cave (paid tour).
  • Allocate ~45 min for the Hraunfossar + Barnafoss pair, plus 15 min if you stop at the cafe.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Tripadvisor rating ~4.5/5 with 1,000+ reviews. Consistently praised as one of the most distinctive waterfalls in Iceland — visitors note that photos do not capture the scale or the way the water seems to materialize from solid rock. Common refrain: "we expected one waterfall and got hundreds."

Honest take: Genuinely underrated. Not on the Golden Circle so it gets a fraction of the traffic, but visually it is more interesting than half the must-see south-coast waterfalls. The 1.5 hr detour from Reykjavik is the main reason it stays quiet — well worth it on Day 6 when you're already up here.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. Optional nearby paid add-on: Vidgelmir lava cave tour (~10 km away) operated by The Cave / Vidgelmir, ~ISK 8,500 ≈ ฿2,125 per adult, advance booking recommended via thecave.is. Skip if short on time on Day 6.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Wooden viewing decks and gravel paths can be icy in April mornings — micro-spikes recommended.
  • Stay on marked paths and behind railings. The cliff edge above the falls is unfenced in places and the drop is fatal.
  • Do not climb down toward the river — the rocks are slippery and the current is strong.
  • No SafeTravel.is alerts specific to Hraunfossar at time of research.
  • Cash for restroom (1,000 ISK).
