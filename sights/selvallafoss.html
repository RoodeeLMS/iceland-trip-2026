Selvallafoss (walk-behind waterfall)

Day 8
📍 Coordinates64.9233, -22.8117
📅 Trip DayDay 8
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Selvallafoss (also spelled Sjávarfoss / Selvallavatnsfoss locally) is a small waterfall on the south flank of Snæfellsnes between Stykkishólmur and Grundarfjörður, near the lake Selvallavatn. It is one of a handful of Icelandic waterfalls you can walk behind — like a miniature Seljalandsfoss without the crowds. There is no official parking lot, no entrance fee, and very little signage. Most visitors walk straight past it. Worth ~20-30 minutes if you are passing on Route 56 / 54 and conditions are dry.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round, free. No facilities. Path can be slippery and the walk-behind is muddy.

April 2026: Spring snowmelt means flow may be higher than the typical "thin curtain" walk-behind photos suggest. If flow is heavy, the walk-behind can be too wet/dangerous — assess on arrival. Path is unmarked and can be icy.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Find the unmarked pull-off on Route 56From Stykkishólmur, drive south on Route 56 toward the Vatnaleið / Grundarfjörður junction. The waterfall is visible from the road on the south-east side near the lake Selvallavatn. There is a small unsigned gravel pull-off — easy to miss. Coordinates: 64.9233, -22.8117. Plug them into the GPS directly.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Pull-off, 64.9233, -22.8117
2
Walk the informal path to the baseFrom the pull-off, an obvious foot trace leads ~150 m across grass and rocks to the base of the falls. No bridges or steps; walking shoes minimum, waterproof boots ideal.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 ~150 m from road
3
Photograph from the front firstStand at the base, ~10 m back from the splash zone. Wide lens (16-35 mm) captures the full curtain. Best light is mid-to-late afternoon.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Front of falls
4
Walk behind the curtain — only if flow is moderateA narrow ledge runs behind the falling water. Assess first: if the flow is heavy or the ledge is icy, do not attempt — turn around. If safe, the walk-behind takes ~30 seconds and gives the iconic "looking out through a curtain of water" view. Camera lens will get wet — bring a cloth and a rain cover or weather-sealed body.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Behind the falls
5
Walk back to the carRetrace the same path. Total stop ~25-30 min.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Back to road

📷 Photography & Drone

  • Front view: 16-24 mm wide, tripod, 1/4 sec shutter to silk the curtain.
  • Walk-behind: 14-20 mm ultra-wide pointing back out through the water. Expose for the bright outside, let the cave wall go dark — silhouette is the shot. Lens cloth essential.
  • Best light: Late afternoon — sun lights the water from outside, walk-behind interior stays in shadow for contrast.

🚁 Drone: No formal restriction here. Small waterfall, low traffic. Standard courtesy applies. See Planning drone section.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 56 to an unsigned gravel pull-off. Any rental car. ~20 min south of Stykkishólmur, ~30 min east of Kirkjufell.

  • Parking: Free, informal pull-off for ~3-4 cars.
  • Walk: ~150 m unmarked footpath, 5 min one way.
  • Fees: None.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 25-30 min total.
  • Best light: Mid-to-late afternoon.
  • Combine with: Stykkishólmur (20 min north), Berserkjahraun (15 min north), Kirkjufell (30 min west). Slot it on the Day 8 loop only if weather is dry.
  • Skip if: Heavy spring flow (walk-behind dangerous), icy approach path, or you are short on time. This is the lowest-priority stop on Day 8.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Few formal reviews — Selvallafoss is genuinely off the beaten track. Reddit r/VisitingIceland threads describe it as "Seljalandsfoss without the crowds" and "worth 20 minutes if you are passing." Honest take: minor sight, only worth the stop if you are already on Route 56 and weather cooperates. If both conditions fail, skip and spend the time at Kirkjufell or Stykkishólmur instead.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Slippery rocks on the approach and behind the curtain.
  • Cold spray — change of clothes optional but smart.
  • Do not attempt the walk-behind in heavy flow or icy conditions — there is no railing and the ledge is narrow.
  • No phone signal at the falls; tell someone where you are.
