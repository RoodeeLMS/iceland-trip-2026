📖 Overview

The Lava Centre in Hvolsvollur is a modern interactive exhibition on Icelandic volcanism, plate tectonics, and earthquakes. Opened in 2017 and significantly upgraded since, it covers Iceland's major volcanic systems with floor-to-ceiling video installations, an earthquake simulator, a 12-minute panoramic film, and a long corridor showing real-time volcanic data. Located right on Route 1 in Hvolsvollur, it's the most polished volcano museum on the south coast and a strong rainy-day option.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 Open year-round — Typically 09:00-19:00 in summer, 09:00-17:00 in shoulder seasons. Verify on lavacentre.is the morning of your visit.

Given Iceland's ongoing volcanic activity (2024-2026 Reykjanes/Sundhnukur eruption series), the live-data corridor and recent eruption coverage are likely to be the highlight currently.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park & enterLarge free parking lot directly off Route 1 in Hvolsvollur. Café and gift shop are accessible without a ticket; the exhibition itself requires a ticket from the reception.
⏱️ 3 min · 📍 Lot, Hvolsvollur
2
Buy ticketsAdult exhibition + film: ~ISK 5,400 ≈ ฿1,350. Exhibition only (no film): ~ISK 3,900 ≈ ฿975. The combined ticket is the recommended option. Check current price on the official site.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Reception
3
Watch the panoramic film firstThe 12-minute film in the auditorium is the orientation — drone footage of major Icelandic volcanic systems and recent eruptions. Watch this before walking the exhibition for context.
⏱️ 12 min · 📍 Cinema
4
Volcanic systems corridorThe main exhibition hallway has stations for each major Icelandic volcano (Hekla, Katla, Eyjafjallajokull, Grimsvotn, etc.) with interactive video panels and physical lava samples. Take your time; the depth here is the value.
⏱️ 30-40 min · 📍 Main exhibition
5
Earthquake & tectonic plate roomDedicated room with a floor that shakes to demonstrate magnitude scale, a model showing the Mid-Atlantic Ridge running through Iceland, and live seismic data from the Icelandic Met Office.
⏱️ 10-15 min · 📍 Earthquake room
6
Live-data corridorLong darkened hall with screens showing real-time volcanic and seismic activity from across Iceland — current alert levels, recent earthquakes, deformation data. Especially interesting given the active Reykjanes eruption series.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Live-data corridor
7
Café & exitThe café/gift shop is accessible to anyone (no ticket needed) — a useful coffee stop even if you skip the exhibition.
⏱️ 15 min · 📍 Café

📷 Photography & Drone

Indoor museum — no specific landscape photography here. Phone snaps of exhibits are fine; large camera setups feel out of place. The exhibition is dark in places (intentional for the video installations) so prepare for high ISO. 🚫 No drones — entirely indoor, no relevance.

🚗 Getting There

Direct on Route 1 in the town of Hvolsvollur, ~100 km from Reykjavik (~1.5h drive), 30 min west of Seljalandsfoss. Free parking, large lot. Any car.

  • Exhibition + film: ~ISK 5,400 ≈ ฿1,350 adult.
  • Exhibition only: ~ISK 3,900 ≈ ฿975 adult.
  • Café/shop: free entry, no ticket needed.
  • Hours: typically 09:00-17:00/19:00 — verify on official site.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Indoor option for a rainy/windy first stop on Day 3, or a context-setting first stop before the waterfalls. Allow 90-120 minutes total including the film.

  • Best timing: Open early — a great first stop on Day 3 before the south coast bus crowds reach Seljalandsfoss.
  • Crowds: Moderate; tour buses include this on rainy days.
  • Combine with: Seljalandsfoss + Gljufrabui (~30 min east); useful "warmup" before driving deeper into the south coast.
  • Time budget: 90-120 min including the film and café.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google rating ~4.4. Reviews are mixed-positive: praise for the production quality, the panoramic film, and the live data; criticism that for ฿1,350 the experience is on the short side (some feel an hour is enough) and that some interactive panels were broken on visit.

Honest take: Worth it if (a) it's raining/windy and outdoor sights are a wash, (b) anyone in the group is interested in geology/volcanoes, or (c) you want context before visiting the volcanic landscapes farther east. If the weather is perfect, the cost-benefit tilts toward skipping it and using the time outdoors.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities. Audio guide included with ticket.

⚠️ Safety Notes

No specific hazards. Indoor museum, fully accessible.
