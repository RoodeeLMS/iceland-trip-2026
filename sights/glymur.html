Glymur Waterfall Hike

Day 6⚠️ Likely SKIP in April
📍 Coordinates64.3925, -21.2414
📅 Trip DayDay 6 (West)
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Glymur is Iceland's second-tallest waterfall at 198 m (held the #1 title until Morsarfoss was discovered after Vatnajokull glacier retreated). It plunges down a narrow basalt canyon at the head of the Hvalfjordur fjord, ~1 hour from Reykjavik. There is no roadside view — reaching it requires a 3-4 hour round-trip hike with steep terrain, a cave/tunnel passage, and a river crossing on a seasonal log bridge. In summer it's one of the country's most rewarding day hikes. In April it is generally closed / not hikeable — see the warning box below.

🚦 Current Status

🔴 LIKELY IMPRACTICAL — APRIL 2026 — Per AllTrails the route is currently flagged "CLOSED" for winter. The seasonal log bridges across the Botnsa river are removed in early October and not replaced until mid-to-late June (per Guide to Iceland and Earth Trekkers). Without the bridges the river is uncrossable. The steep canyon-edge sections are also typically snow- and ice-covered in April, with documented fatal falls in shoulder seasons.

Recommendation for our April 8-17 trip: Skip Glymur as a hike. Optionally drive to the trailhead parking lot for the 5 minute view of the canyon mouth and the Hvalfjordur fjord scenery, but do not attempt the trail. Save Glymur for a future summer visit (June-September).

The page below documents the full hike for that future reference.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step (summer reference)

1
Park at the Botnsdalur trailhead Free dirt-and-gravel lot at the end of Route 47, deep in Hvalfjordur. ~1 hr from Reykjavik. There is a basic pit toilet and a trailhead sign with a map. Get there early — the lot fills by 10:00 in summer.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Botnsdalur lot, end of Rd 47
2
Choose the south or north loop direction The trail is a loop (~7 km total). The standard direction is up the south side first, cross at the top, return on the north. Going clockwise puts the steep cave section on the way up when you're fresh. Total hike: 3-4 hours, 350 m elevation gain.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Trail fork ~200 m from lot
3
First river crossing — the lower log 20 min in, you reach the first Botnsa crossing: a single log laid across the river with a steel cable to hold for balance. Take off boots and wade if you don't trust the log — the water is shin to knee deep in summer. This log is removed October-mid-June.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Botnsa lower crossing, ~1.5 km in
4
The cave passage (Þvottahellir) The trail enters a short natural cave / lava tube that you walk through to emerge on the canyon's south face. Headlamp not required but a phone torch helps. This is where the real climb begins.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 Cave entrance ~2 km in
5
Steep canyon-edge climb to the south viewpoint Switchbacks up the south wall of the canyon with some fixed ropes and chains on the steepest sections. Stay back from the unfenced edge — the drop is several hundred metres. Around the halfway mark you get the first full view of Glymur's main 198 m drop with the canyon framing it. This is the iconic shot.
⏱️ 60-75 min · 📍 South canyon edge
6
Top of the falls — second river crossing At the top of the canyon you reach the upper Botnsa crossing — a wider, shallower wade across the river just above where it pours over the lip. Boots off, sandals or barefoot, ~10-15 cm deep in summer. Also seasonal/uncrossable in winter.
⏱️ 15 min · 📍 Top of falls, ~3.5 km in
7
North side descent and viewpoints Cross to the north rim and descend back to the lot via a gentler trail. Multiple side viewpoints look back across the canyon at the falls — the late-afternoon light here is the best of the entire hike. ~1.5 hr down.
⏱️ 90 min · 📍 North rim trail
8
April alternative — drive-up canyon mouth view Even when the hike is closed, the drive into Hvalfjordur is scenic and the trailhead lot is worth a 10 min stop. You won't see the falls themselves (they are deep in the canyon) but you'll see the lower river and the canyon entrance. Then turn around and continue Day 6 north toward Hraunfossar.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Botnsdalur lot only

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions (if hiking in summer):

  • South-rim half-height viewpoint: The full 198 m drop framed by the V of the canyon. 24-50 mm. Best around 13:00-15:00 when sun lights the falls inside the canyon (it's in shade most of the day).
  • North rim looking back across: Late afternoon side-light on the canyon walls and the falls in the background. Best single composition of the loop.
  • Telephoto detail: 70-200 mm to isolate the upper plunge against the dark basalt. The water plumes and disperses dramatically as it falls.
  • Lens choice: Wide for context, tele for the falls themselves — the canyon is too narrow for a single lens to do both.

🚁 Drone: Not in a national park. No specific local ban posted, but the canyon walls funnel wind unpredictably and there is no recovery if you crash into the gorge. If flying, launch from the upper rim with VLOS, not from the canyon floor. For general Iceland drone law see the Drone section on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

~1 hour from Reykjavik. Take Route 1 north, exit onto Route 47 around the Hvalfjordur fjord, follow it to the very end at Botnsdalur. The last few km are paved but narrow.

  • Parking: Free dirt lot at the trailhead.
  • Trail length: ~7 km loop, 3-4 hours, 350 m elevation gain.
  • Difficulty: Strenuous. Steep sections with fixed ropes/chains. Two river crossings (summer only).
  • Entry: Free.
  • Vehicle: Any 2WD rental fine to the lot; the hike does the rest.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

For our trip: Skip the hike. Optional 10 min stop at the trailhead lot if Day 6 timing allows, otherwise drive past and prioritise Hraunfossar / Barnafoss / Deildartunguhver.

For a future summer visit:

  • Best season: Late June to mid-September. Log bridges are confirmed in place by late June.
  • Best light: 13:00-15:00 for sun inside the canyon; late afternoon for north-rim side-light.
  • Crowds: Busy in July/August on weekends — start before 9:00 to beat the rush and have parking.
  • Combine with: Could be a half-day from Reykjavik on its own, or paired with Hvalfjordur fjord drive.
  • Allocate: 5 hours total (drive + 4 hour hike).

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

AllTrails 4.7/5 with 2,400+ reviews — one of Iceland's top-rated day hikes. Reviewers consistently call it the most rewarding hike near Reykjavik and praise the variety: river crossings, cave passage, canyon views, and the falls themselves. Common warnings: steeper than people expect, river crossings are no joke, and the canyon-edge sections are exposed.

Honest take: Genuinely top-tier when in season. The reason it's not on every Iceland highlight list is the ~4 hour time commitment — many tourists don't have a half-day to spare for a single waterfall. For us in April it is unfortunately just not viable. Bookmark for summer.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided. Guided hikes are offered by some Reykjavik operators (e.g. Arctic Adventures, Hvammsvik) for ~ISK 18,000-25,000 ≈ ฿4,500-6,250 per person but are unnecessary in summer if you're a fit hiker. Not relevant for our April visit (no operators run it in April either).

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • April warning: Trail is effectively closed. Snow and ice on canyon-edge sections, no log bridges, freezing river. Multiple injuries and at least one fatal fall have occurred in shoulder seasons. Do not attempt.
  • Summer hike risks: Steep unfenced canyon edges, slippery wet rock, fast-moving river crossings. Wear hiking boots with grip; trekking poles strongly recommended.
  • No phone signal in most of the canyon — tell someone your plan.
  • Even in summer, weather can deteriorate fast — bring layers and a rain shell.
  • Per SafeTravel.is: file a travel plan if hiking off-season.
