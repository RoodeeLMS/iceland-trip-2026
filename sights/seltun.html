Seltún Geothermal Area (Krýsuvík)

Day 9
📍 Coordinates63.8978, -22.0525
📅 Trip DayDay 9 ★
📍 Open in Google Maps

📖 Overview

Seltún is the most accessible part of the Krýsuvík high-temperature geothermal field, a stretch of bubbling mud pots, fumaroles, and brilliantly coloured mineral ground on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge. A wooden boardwalk loop lets you walk safely across ground that is 80-100°C just below the surface. The yellow, orange, and rust-red mineral colours come from sulphur, iron oxides, and mineral deposits — at this scale and intensity it's the closest you'll get to Geysir or Hverir without driving the Ring Road.

🚦 Current Status

🟠 Krýsuvík subsidence reported by IMO — Per the Icelandic Met Office, faster subsidence has been recorded in the Krýsuvík area in recent months alongside the ongoing magma accumulation under Svartsengi (~21 million m³). The site itself remains open and the boardwalks intact, but the geothermal field is sensitive to seismic shifts. Confirm on vedur.is and safetravel.is the morning of your visit.

The site is open 24/7, free, no booking required. There's a small gravel parking lot, an interpretive sign, and the boardwalk loop. New steam vents have opened spontaneously in the past — boardwalk routing has been adjusted multiple times. April conditions: ground will be muddy at the lot edges, sulphur smell strong, and the contrast between cold air and hot steam makes for dramatic plumes. Daylight ~14 hours.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park and read the warning sign Small free gravel lot directly off Route 42. The interpretive board explains what you're seeing and (more importantly) why you must stay on the boardwalk. Cover your face if you're sensitive to sulphur — the smell is strong and instant.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Trailhead off Route 42
2
Walk the lower boardwalk past the mud pots The first 50 m of boardwalk passes the largest cluster of grey-blue bubbling mud pots. They glop and pop irregularly — stand and watch one for a full minute and you'll catch a few good plops. This is the best video clip of the visit.
⏱️ 5-8 min · 📍 First 50 m of boardwalk
3
Cross the central section — yellow and orange mineral ground Past the mud pots the boardwalk skirts a slope of brilliant yellow sulphur and orange iron-oxide deposits. This is the photogenic core. Steam vents (fumaroles) hiss continuously from cracks. If the wind is from the south, plumes drift across the boardwalk and you walk through warm steam.
⏱️ 10 min · 📍 Mid loop, mineral slope
4
Climb to the upper viewing platform A short boardwalk staircase climbs to a platform on the hillside above the field. Best top-down composition of the whole site — the colour patches read clearly from above. Stay 5 minutes for the view, longer if light is good.
⏱️ 8 min · 📍 Upper platform, 30 m above field
5
Loop back via the eastern stretch Descend the stairs and complete the loop along the eastern boardwalk past smaller hot springs. Look for the small bright-blue pool near the end — it's just clear water but the mineral floor reflects an intense turquoise.
⏱️ 5 min · 📍 East loop back to lot

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • Top-down from the upper platform: 24-50 mm. The colour patches read like an abstract painting from above — the strongest single shot here.
  • Steam plume backlighting: Walk the boardwalk so the sun is in front of you and shoot fumaroles against the dark hillside. Steam glows. Mid-morning works.
  • Mud pot macro: 70-200 mm tele or any macro — get a tight crop on a bubbling pot mid-plop. Burst mode helps.
  • Cold-air contrast: April mornings are cold enough that the steam plumes are huge. Best dramatic shots are before 11:00.

🚁 Drone — DO NOT FLY OVER THE GEOTHERMAL FIELD. Steam plumes are corrosive and will damage motors and electronics within minutes; you also risk disturbing other visitors and breaching local site rules. Per Visit Reykjanes site signage, drones are discouraged at active geothermal sites. If you must fly, do it from the parking area looking out over Kleifarvatn or the surrounding hills, not over the field. General drone rules on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 42 (Krýsuvíkurvegur), 5 minutes south of Kleifarvatn. About 35 minutes from Reykjavík. Any 2WD rental.

  • Parking: Free gravel lot directly at the trailhead.
  • Walk: ~30 seconds from car to first boardwalk.
  • Fees: None.
  • Facilities: No toilets on-site as of 2026 (some 2025 reviews mention portable toilets installed seasonally — not guaranteed). Use Hafnarfjörður before driving south.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Priority ★ sight — the visual highlight of the eastern Reykjanes loop and the easiest "real" geothermal field experience near Reykjavík.

  • Best light: Mid-morning (9:00-11:00) for backlit steam plumes and side light on mineral colours.
  • Crowds: Quiet outside summer high season. Day-trip buses from Reykjavík sometimes pile in 11:00-13:00 — go before or after.
  • Combine with: Kleifarvatn (5 min north) and Grindavík/Brimketill/Gunnuhver (continuing west and south).
  • Allocate: ~30 minutes for the full boardwalk loop. 45 if photographing seriously.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Tripadvisor and Google reviewers rate it ~4.4/5. Common praise: "more interesting than expected," "great smell of sulphur," and the colours. Common complaints: short visit, can be crowded around bus arrivals, and complaints about people stepping off the boardwalk for selfies (don't — the ground will burn through your shoes). Honest take: not overrated. It's a small field but the colour intensity and accessibility are excellent for a free, 30-minute stop.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Self-guided — no booked activities needed. The boardwalk loop is the entire visit.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Stay on the boardwalk at all times. Ground temperatures reach 80-100°C just below a thin crust. People have suffered severe burns by stepping off.
  • Sulphur dioxide and hydrogen sulphide are present. If you have asthma or heart conditions, limit exposure and stay upwind of plumes.
  • New vents can open spontaneously — if a section of boardwalk is taped off, do not cross.
  • Active volcanic monitoring on the peninsula (Svartsengi/Krýsuvík). Check vedur.is and safetravel.is the morning of your visit.
  • Do not let children or pets off the boardwalk.
