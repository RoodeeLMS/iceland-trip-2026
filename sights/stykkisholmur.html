Stykkishólmur Harbor Town

Day 8
📍 Coordinates65.0767, -22.7269
📅 Trip DayDay 8
📍 Open in Google Maps🥾 Walking directions

📖 Overview

Stykkishólmur is a working fishing town of ~1,200 people on the north-east corner of the Snæfellsnes peninsula, with a colourful harbour, a basalt-island viewpoint (Súgandisey), a 19th-century Norwegian merchant's house museum, and the conceptual art installation Library of Water by Roni Horn. Movie buffs will recognise it as a key location from The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (2013). Björk filmed her early career music videos here. It is also the ferry port for Flatey island in Breiðafjörður bay (ferry runs to Brjánslækur in the Westfjords). For our trip it is mostly a 60-90 min lunch + walk + view stop on the Day 8 loop.

🚦 Current Status

🟢 All open year-round. Library of Water and Norwegian House have reduced winter hours (typically Tue-Sat afternoons in April — verify on the day). Ferry to Flatey runs daily but is impractical as a same-day visit.

April 2026: Easter holidays may shift museum hours. Town is fully functional and open.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

1
Park at the harbour lotFree public parking on the harbour-front by the ferry terminal. Multiple lots within 200 m of each other. Easy access from Route 58.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Harbour, 65.0767, -22.7269
2
Walk the harbour and photograph the colourful boatsStykkishólmur's working fishing harbour has the bright red, blue and green hulls that make every Snæfellsnes brochure cover. Walk the full length of the quay (~200 m). The Sæferðir ferry to Flatey docks at the far end.
⏱️ 15 min · 📍 Harbour quay
3
Climb Súgandisey for the panoramic viewAt the north end of the harbour, a wooden footbridge crosses to the small basalt island of Súgandisey. A short ~5 min walk up the path leads to a small orange lighthouse and a 360° view over the town, the harbour, the islands of Breiðafjörður, and (clear days) the mountains of the Westfjords across the bay. This is the headline view of Stykkishólmur — do not skip it.
⏱️ 20-25 min · 📍 Súgandisey, ~5 min walk + view time
4
Visit the Library of Water (Vatnasafn)5 min walk uphill from the harbour, the Library of Water is a former library building converted by American artist Roni Horn into an installation: 24 floor-to-ceiling glass columns each filled with water from a different Icelandic glacier. Quiet, contemplative, ~15 min visit. Small admission ~ISK 1,500 ≈ ฿375. Verify hours.
⏱️ 20-25 min · 📍 Bókhlöðustígur, ~400 m from harbour
5
Walk the old town (Norska húsið)The 1832 Norwegian House (Norska húsið) is the oldest two-storey building in Iceland and now a regional folk museum (~ISK 1,500 ≈ ฿375). The streets around it have well-preserved 19th-century timber houses painted in saturated colours. Even if you skip the museum interior, the 10-minute walk around the block is worth it.
⏱️ 20-30 min · 📍 Old town, ~300 m from harbour
6
Lunch stopSeveral solid options: Sjávarpakkhúsið (harbour-front, seafood), Narfeyrarstofa (more upscale), Skúrinn (casual fish & chips). All in the harbour/old town area within 5 min walk.
⏱️ 60-90 min · 📍 Harbour area
7
Refuel groceries at Bonus or Krónan (optional)Stykkishólmur has a Bonus supermarket on the south edge of town (Route 58 in) — useful for stocking snacks/drinks for the rest of the Day 8 loop. Open daily 11:00-18:30 typical hours.
⏱️ 15 min · 📍 Bonus, edge of town

📷 Photography & Drone

  • From Súgandisey: Wide 16-24 mm panorama looking south-west over the harbour and town with the mountains behind. The single best Stykkishólmur shot.
  • Boat colour close-ups: 35-85 mm tight on red/blue/yellow hulls reflected in calm harbour water.
  • Old town houses: 35-50 mm of the painted timber facades — best in soft overcast light.
  • Best light: Late afternoon for the harbour (south-west facing), morning for Súgandisey.

🚁 Drone: Stykkishólmur is a town — keep clear of people and the harbour traffic. No formal town-wide ban that we found, but treat as urban airspace and fly only over open water/empty areas with caution. The Súgandisey lighthouse is a small protected area; do not buzz nesting birds in spring. See Planning drone section.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 58 directly into town. Any rental car. ~40 min east of Kirkjufell, ~1.5 hours from Reykjavik.

  • Parking: Free at the harbour. Multiple lots.
  • Walk: Everything in the centre is within 5-10 min walk of the harbour.
  • Fees: Library of Water and Norwegian House each ~฿375.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

  • Allocate: 90 min minimum (harbour + Súgandisey + lunch); 2-3 hours if doing Library of Water and Norwegian House too.
  • Best time: Late morning to early afternoon for museum hours and lunch.
  • Combine with: Berserkjahraun (15 min south on Route 56), Selvallafoss (25 min south-west), and Kirkjufell (40 min west) — the Day 8 loop.
  • Skip the Flatey ferry: day-trip works in summer with multiple sailings; in April only one daily round trip and the schedule does not give enough island time. Skip for our trip.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Google Maps ~4.6 for the town as a whole. Praised as a charming, authentic working fishing town — not a tourist trap. Súgandisey viewpoint is universally rated as the highlight. Library of Water is "love it or shrug" depending on taste for conceptual art. Honest take: the most "real town" stop on Snæfellsnes. Not a bucket-list destination on its own but a legitimate 90-min stop with a great view, decent food, and a useful supermarket.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

Mostly self-guided. Optional bookings:

  • Sæferðir / Seatours — Breiðafjörður island and bird-watching boat tours from the harbour. ~฿4,000-7,000 depending on tour. seatours.is.
  • Library of Water: walk-in, ~฿375.
  • Norwegian House Museum: walk-in, ~฿375.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Súgandisey footbridge can be slippery in wet/icy conditions.
  • Working harbour — watch for vehicles, ropes, and ferry traffic.
  • Standard town safety; nothing unusual.
