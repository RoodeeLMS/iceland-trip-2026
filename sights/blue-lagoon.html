📖 Overview

The Blue Lagoon is Iceland's most famous tourist site: a milky pale-blue silica-rich geothermal pool set inside a black lava field, fed by waste water from the adjacent Svartsengi geothermal power plant. The lagoon sits in the heart of the Sundhnúkur volcanic system that has erupted multiple times since late 2023, and the entire complex (Blue Lagoon, Silica Hotel, Retreat) has been evacuated and closed several times during eruptions. It's a slick, expensive, well-photographed spa experience for those who want to bathe — and a slightly awkward visit for anyone who doesn't.

⚠️ Group note: Our group does not bathe. The Blue Lagoon's entire value proposition is the bathing experience — without that, the visit is mostly photographs from the viewing path and possibly a meal at LAVA Restaurant. Honest call: probably skip. Read on so you can make your own decision.

🚦 Current Status

🟠 Reykjanes volcanic system on Heightened Unrest (Level 2) — Per the Icelandic Met Office, no eruption underway in April 2026. The most recent Sundhnúkur eruption ended August 2025; ~21 million m³ of magma has accumulated under Svartsengi since. Blue Lagoon is currently open (per icelandia.com 2026 status updates), Grindavík is partially repopulated, and the access road (Route 426) is open. All of this can change in hours if a new eruption begins. Confirm on bluelagoon.com and safetravel.is the day before and morning of your visit.

Reservations are required even just to enter the site if you want to bathe — walk-ups are turned away. Last entry is typically ~1 hour before closing. April 2026 daylight ~14 hours; the lagoon is open from morning to evening with timed-entry slots.

🚶 Your Visit — Step by Step

This walk-through assumes you are not bathing — exterior visit + LAVA Restaurant or Moss café option only.

1
Decide before you leave: are you eating at LAVA? LAVA Restaurant is the one part of the complex you can experience meaningfully without bathing. Reservations are required and should be made online days in advance. Lunch mains run ISK 4,500-4,900 (~฿1,125-1,225); a 3-course dinner is around ISK 7,600 (~฿1,900). The restaurant is built into an 800-year-old lava cliff, with windows overlooking the lagoon. If you're not booking a meal, the visit is essentially a 30-minute exterior photo stop. Decide now whether the trip is worth it.
⏱️ Pre-trip · 📍 bluelagoon.com/restaurant/lava
2
Drive in via Route 426 — pause at the lava-field viewpoint The access road from Route 43 winds through a young black lava field. Pull off at any of the small lay-bys for a photo of the road snaking through the lava — this is one of the more cinematic 5 minutes of driving on Reykjanes. Look north for the Sundhnúkur crater row on the horizon (where the recent eruptions came from).
⏱️ 5-10 min · 📍 Route 426 lava-field stretch
3
Park at the main lot (free) Large free lot at the entrance complex. From here you can walk the public paths on the lava ridge that overlook the lagoon — no ticket required. This is the part you can do for free.
⏱️ 2 min · 📍 Main visitor parking
4
Walk the public viewing paths over the lava ridge A short path leads from the main entrance up onto the lava ridge above the lagoon. From here you can see the milky-blue pool against the black lava — the iconic image. No fee, no booking required. This is where 90% of the photographic value lives. ~15-20 minutes is enough.
⏱️ 15-20 min · 📍 Public paths over the lava ridge
5
Optional: Moss Café / Blue Café (no booking needed) The complex has small cafés that don't require a lagoon ticket. Coffee, sandwiches, soup. Not memorable, but a warm refuge from the wind. Check at the entrance reception which café is currently open to non-bathers — Blue Lagoon's policy on non-bathing visitors has shifted year to year.
⏱️ 30 min · 📍 Entrance complex
6
Optional: LAVA Restaurant (booked in advance) If you booked, your meal is the main event. Tasting menu and à la carte both available. The dining room and lagoon view are the actual draw — food reviews are mixed (~4.0-4.2/5 on Google), mostly fine, occasionally great, always expensive. Allow 1.5-2 hours.
⏱️ 90-120 min · 📍 LAVA Restaurant inside complex
7
Verdict if not bathing or eating: 30-minute stop maximum If you're neither bathing nor eating at LAVA, treat this as a quick scenic stop on the drive between the rest of Reykjanes and Reykjavík/KEF. Drive in, walk the public paths, take your photos, leave. There is no entrance ticket for this — and there is also not much else to do.
⏱️ 30 min total · 📍 Public paths only

📷 Photography & Drone

Best compositions:

  • From the public lava ridge path: 24-50 mm capturing the pool curve against black lava and the spa buildings. This is the iconic shot and you can take it without a ticket.
  • Steam-over-water: Cold mornings produce huge steam plumes. 70-200 mm tele compresses people and steam into a soft, painterly layer.
  • Driving-in cinematic: From a lay-by on Route 426 with the lava field foreground and the spa steam plume on the horizon.

🚁 Drone — DO NOT FLY HERE. The Blue Lagoon and Svartsengi power plant complex is a no-fly zone for civilian drones (privacy of bathers, security of the power plant, plus ongoing volcanic-event TFRs across the peninsula). Don't even launch from the parking lot. General drone rules on the Planning page.

🚗 Getting There

Paved Route 43 then Route 426. About 50 minutes from Reykjavík, 20 minutes from KEF airport — the location makes it a natural last-stop on the drive to your departing flight (which is exactly when most tour buses arrive). Any 2WD rental.

  • Parking: Free, large lot at the entrance complex.
  • Walk: ~1-2 min from lot to entrance / public viewing paths.
  • Fees: Lagoon entry only (see pricing tiers below). No fee for parking, walking the public paths, or visiting the entrance/cafés.
  • Facilities: Full visitor centre, multiple cafés, restaurants, gift shop, toilets.

💰 Pricing Tiers (for reference, if you change your mind)

  • Comfort: from ISK 9,990 (~฿2,500). Includes entry, silica mud mask, towel, one drink. No bathrobe or slippers.
  • Premium: from ISK 14,990 (~฿3,750). Adds bathrobe, slippers, two extra masks, second drink, sparkling wine if dining at LAVA (skip — group doesn't drink).
  • Signature: from ISK 18,490 (~฿4,625). Adds take-home skincare products.
  • Retreat Spa: separate facility, much more expensive (฿20,000+). Limited capacity, terraced lava pools, no phones.
  • LAVA Restaurant: 2-course ~ISK 6,600 (~฿1,650), 3-course ~ISK 7,600 (~฿1,900). Mains à la carte ISK 4,500-4,900 (~฿1,125-1,225). Reservations required, no lagoon ticket needed.

Source: bluelagoon.com and LAVA Restaurant page, 2026. Conversion at ฿0.25/ISK.

🗺️ Itinerary & Timing

Day 9 ★ on the original plan. Geographically, Blue Lagoon is the natural last stop on the Reykjanes loop and a logical detour on the way back to Reykjavík or to KEF airport. Timing the visit to your departing flight is the classic move — even without bathing, a LAVA lunch + 30 min on the public paths fits cleanly into a transfer day.

  • Best light: Early morning or late afternoon for warm low light on the lagoon and steam plumes.
  • Crowds: Always busy. Quietest at first slot of the day.
  • Combine with: Grindavík (5 min south), Brimketill, Gunnuhver, Reykjanesviti — make Blue Lagoon the last stop of the loop.
  • Allocate (not bathing): 30-45 minutes for paths only; 2-2.5 hours including LAVA Restaurant.

⭐ Reviews & Reputation

Tripadvisor and Google reviews are mixed: ~4.2/5 with thousands of reviews. Praise: the lagoon itself is genuinely striking, facilities are slick, silica masks feel good. Criticism: expensive, crowded, processed, and several reviews note that Sky Lagoon (closer to Reykjavík) and Mývatn Nature Baths offer a better value-for-experience ratio.

Honest take for our group: The Blue Lagoon is built around bathing. Take that out and what's left is a power-plant-fed pool you can photograph for free from the public path. The food at LAVA is fine but not a destination meal. Recommended action: drive past on the way to KEF, stop for 30 minutes on the public paths, take the iconic photo, and continue. Skip the ticket. Skip the LAVA dinner unless you specifically want a sit-down meal in a dramatic room.

🎟️ Activities & Providers

  • Day visit (bathing): Book direct at bluelagoon.com. Reservation required. Group not bathing — skip.
  • LAVA Restaurant: Book direct at bluelagoon.com/restaurant/lava. No lagoon ticket needed. Optional for our group.
  • Public viewing paths: Free, no booking. Open during the complex's operating hours.
  • Bus transfer from Reykjavík: Reykjavík Excursions and Flybus run direct shuttles. Not relevant — we have the rental car.

⚠️ Safety Notes

  • Volcanic activity is the main consideration. The Blue Lagoon and Grindavík have been evacuated multiple times since late 2023 during Sundhnúkur eruptions. Check vedur.is and safetravel.is the day before and the morning of your visit. If a new eruption begins, do not enter the area.
  • The access road can be closed at very short notice — have a backup plan (drive directly to KEF or back to Reykjavík).
  • The lava field around the complex is sharp and uneven; stay on marked paths.
  • Standard hot-water cautions if anyone in the group changes their mind: silica is rough on jewellery (remove silver) and on hair (apply conditioner before entering).
  • Strong winds across the open lava field — windproof outer layer required.
