Iceland Trip 2026 - Detailed Day-by-Day Plan

Important Considerations for an April Trip

  • Weather & Flexibility: April in Iceland is unpredictable. You can experience winter and spring in the same day. This itinerary is a plan, not a promise. Always check the daily forecast on en.vedur.is and road conditions on road.is before driving. Be prepared to change plans, skip a location, or alter your route for safety due to wind, snow, or road closures. Dress in layers and always have waterproof clothing, warm hats, gloves, and sturdy boots.
  • Viewing the Northern Lights: You are visiting at the very end of the aurora season. As the nights are getting shorter, your chances are reduced, but it's still possible! For a sighting, you need three things: darkness, clear skies, and solar activity. The plan maximizes your chances by having you stay in rural areas away from city lights. Check the aurora forecast on en.vedur.is each evening. Consider it a magical bonus if you see them!

Day 1 — Wed Apr 8: Arrival & Reykjavík Exploration

Hallgrímskirkja in Reykjavík

  • Morning (11:00 - 12:30): Arrive at Keflavík (KEF), clear immigration, and pick up the rental car.
  • Afternoon (12:30 - 14:00): Drive directly to Reykjavík (approx. 50 min drive) and check into your accommodation.

Day 2 — Thu Apr 9: The Golden Circle

Sunrise: ~06:22 | Sunset: ~20:23

View Day 2 Route on Google Maps

Þingvellir National Park

Strokkur Geyser

Gullfoss Waterfall

  • Morning (09:00 - 12:00): After breakfast in Reykjavík, drive to Þingvellir National Park (approx. 50 min drive). Walk between the tectonic plates and see Öxarárfoss waterfall.
  • Afternoon (12:00 - 16:00): Continue to the Geysir geothermal area (approx. 1 hour drive) to watch Strokkur erupt, followed by a visit to the magnificent Gullfoss waterfall (approx. 10 min drive).
  • Late Afternoon (16:00 - 18:00): Make a final stop at the Kerið crater (approx. 40 min drive) to see its striking volcanic colors.
  • Evening (18:00 onwards): Drive to your accommodation in the Hvolsvöllur/Hella area (approx. 1 hour drive) to get a head start on the South Coast tomorrow. Have dinner and relax.

Accommodation Areas: Hvolsvöllur / Hella

This is a great area to find a beautiful, private cabin or cottage.

AreaProsCons
Rural Countryside Cabins (off the main road)Often very scenic, beautiful, and private with great kitchens. Ideal for seeing Northern Lights due to low light pollution. Many modern 2-bedroom options.Requires driving on smaller roads. You must buy groceries in a larger town (like Selfoss or Hella) beforehand.
Near Hella or Hvolsvöllur TownsConvenient for accessing supermarkets and gas stations. Easier to find after a long day of driving.Less scenic and private compared to the countryside. The properties might be more functional than beautiful.

Notes: Starting from Reykjavík adds a bit of driving, but staying near Hella/Hvolsvöllur positions you perfectly for the next day's waterfall visits. If the sky is clear tonight, it's your first good chance to look for the Northern Lights!

Day 3 — Fri Apr 10: Waterfalls & Black Sand Beaches

Sunrise: ~06:18 | Sunset: ~20:26

View Day 3 Route on Google Maps

Seljalandsfoss

Skógafoss

Reynisfjara Beach

  • Morning (09:00 - 12:00): From your accommodation, it's a short drive to the iconic Seljalandsfoss (approx. 20 min drive). Don't miss the nearby hidden gem, Gljúfrabúi. Continue to the powerful Skógafoss waterfall (approx. 25 min drive).

Reykjadalur Valley

  • Morning (09:00 - 13:30): Drive from Vík towards Hveragerði (approx. 2 hours). Stop for the memorable Reykjadalur hot river hike. The hike is ~1 hour each way, plus time to bathe in the naturally hot river.

Djúpalónssandur

Kirkjufell
