🌦️ Weather strategy:

Operators auto-cancel + refund if conditions exceed their safety thresholds. Most have 24-48h free cancel for you.
• Day 4 and Day 5 are both glacier-corridor days — every east-coast activity has 2 natural attempts with zero backtracking, because Day 4 drives east (Vík→Hali) and Day 5 drives west (Hali→Hvolsvöllur) through the same route.
• Katla Ice Cave: Book T-48h — Day 5 westward drive passes Vík again so it's a real backup.
• Láki / Sólheimajökull: Book morning-of — genuine multi-day flex.
• Elding: Walk-up only — decide morning of Day 9.

🤿 Silfra Snorkeling Between Continents
🟡 Interesting but cold

One of Iceland's most iconic bucket-list activities. Snorkel in the Silfra fissure between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates at Þingvellir. Visibility is over 100 m — reputed to be the clearest freshwater on Earth. Dry suit keeps your body dry but face, lips, and hands are in direct contact with 2°C (35°F) glacial meltwater. Photos included in the package.

🕐 Departures (verified Apr 6 2026 from dive.is): Multiple daily start times — "meet on location" at Silfra P5 parking lot at Þingvellir. Typical slots every ~30 min from 09:00-14:00 in spring. Fits Day 2 morning before Geysir/Gullfoss.
⚠️ Cold-water warning:

Your group has already ruled out hot spring bathing (Reykjadalur) and Blue Lagoon swimming. Silfra is dramatically colder — you're floating in 2°C water for 30-40 min with face/hands directly exposed. Most cold-sensitive tourists report it as "brain-freeze levels of uncomfortable." If you skipped warm-water bathing, you'll almost certainly hate this. Only book if 1-2 people in the group are individually cold-tolerant and want to do it — not a group activity for this trip.

♨️ Sky Lagoon — 7-Step Ritual
🟡 Book 1-2 days ahead

Reykjavik's premium oceanfront geothermal spa with a famous infinity edge that blends straight into the North Atlantic. The signature 7-Step Ritual alternates hot lagoon → cold plunge → sauna → cold mist → body scrub → steam → shower. Less crowded and arguably more atmospheric than the Blue Lagoon, and only 15 min from Reykjavik centre. Excellent rainy-day or post-arrival decompress option.

🕐 Open: Daily ~09:00–22:00 (varies by season). Booking is timed-entry — pick a slot. Sunset slots (~19:00) are most popular. Book via skylagoon.com.

♨️ Hvammsvík Hot Springs — Hvalfjörður
🟡 Book 1 day ahead

Eight natural hot pools terraced down to the edge of Hvalfjörður fjord — you can swim from a hot pool straight into the cold ocean. Far quieter than Blue Lagoon or Sky Lagoon, set in a working farm/historic estate. Sits directly on the Day 6 route between Reykjavik and Borgarnes (a small detour off Route 1 onto Route 47). One of the most scenic geothermal experiences in Iceland.

🕐 Open: Daily 11:00–21:00. Timed entry. Book via hvammsvik.com.

🕳️ Vatnshellir Lava Cave — Snæfellsnes
🟢 Walk-in usually OK

Descend 8,000-year-old lava tube on Snæfellsnes peninsula. Guided 45-minute tour goes 35 metres underground via a spiral staircase into a perfectly preserved lava channel. Helmets and headlamps provided. Year-round, weather-independent — perfect rainy-day backup for Day 7 if Snæfellsnes is socked in. Right next to Saxhóll crater and Djúpalónssandur black beach.

🕐 Departures: Hourly 10:00–18:00 daily. Walk-in usually fine off-peak; book ahead in summer. Via summitadventureguides.is.

🧊 Into the Glacier — Langjökull Ice Tunnel
🟡 Book 2-3 days ahead

A man-made 500m tunnel carved into Langjökull, Iceland's second-largest glacier. You're transported up onto the ice cap by a converted missile-launcher truck, then walk through a lit tunnel system with side chambers, an ice chapel, and crevasse views. Year-round and not weather-dependent inside the cave — but the truck ride up requires safe surface conditions. Fits the Day 6 westward route (departure point Húsafell, near Hraunfossar).

🕐 Departures: Daily 10:00 and 13:00 from Húsafell base camp (April). Book via intotheglacier.is.
