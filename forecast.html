Your Route Proximity

Day 8 (Apr 16) — Your Reykjanes/Blue Lagoon route passes 5-10 km from eruption sites. Sundhnúkur crater row is between Grindavík and Blue Lagoon.

If an eruption starts during your trip:
  • These are fissure eruptions (lava from ground cracks) — not explosive, generally safe to observe from distance
  • Designated viewing areas are set up at safe distances within hours
  • ⚠️ Volcanic gases (SO₂) are the main danger — always stay upwind, check gas forecasts
  • Roads near Grindavík/Blue Lagoon may be temporarily closed — have a Day 8 backup plan
  • Tour operators launch eruption tours within hours — check Arctic Adventures or Reykjavik Excursions
Even without an active eruption:
  • Visit the 2023-2025 lava fields at Fagradalsfjall — still steaming, hiking trails usually open
  • Fresh black lava against green moss is an incredible photography opportunity
  • The Reykjanes Geopark has geothermal areas, hot springs, and volcanic landscapes everywhere
