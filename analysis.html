üéí Smart Packing Guide

Layering System for Iceland

Layer 1: Base Layers

  • Thermal underwear (top & bottom)
  • Merino wool or synthetic (NOT cotton)
  • Bring 2-3 sets

Layer 2: Mid Layers

  • Fleece jacket or pullover
  • Lightweight down jacket
  • Long-sleeve shirts

Layer 3: Outer Shell

  • ESSENTIAL: Waterproof, windproof jacket with hood
  • Waterproof pants (for waterfalls and rain)
  • This is your #1 most important clothing item

Footwear

  • ESSENTIAL: Waterproof hiking boots with good tread
  • Break them in before the trip!
  • Wool hiking socks (3-4 pairs)
  • Optional: Sandals for hot springs/hotel

Accessories

  • Warm hat/beanie
  • Gloves (even in summer for glaciers)
  • Sunglasses (very bright in summer)
  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+, strong sun reflection)
  • Eye mask (for midnight sun)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Small backpack for day hikes

Tech & Extras

  • Phone charger + car adapter
  • Camera + extra batteries (cold drains batteries)
  • Power bank
  • European plug adapter (Type C/F)
  • Headphones/earbuds

Toiletries & Misc

  • Swimsuit (for hot springs, Blue Lagoon, and Reykjadalur hot river)
  • Quick-dry towel (ESSENTIAL for Reykjadalur hike on Day 6)
  • Small backpack for Reykjadalur with waterproof bag for dry clothes
  • Toiletries (minimize liquids for flights)
  • First aid kit basics
  • Medications + copies of prescriptions
  • Snacks for long drives

What NOT to Bring

  • ‚ùå Cotton clothing (stays wet, doesn't insulate)
  • ‚ùå Umbrella (useless in Iceland wind, wear a hood)
  • ‚ùå Too many clothes (laundry available, pack light)
  • ‚ùå Formal attire (Iceland is casual everywhere)
  • ‚ùå Hair dryer (hotels provide them)
